    Sony WH-1000XM5 vs Sennheiser Momentum 4 Wireless

    Both flagship wireless headphones, but optimized for completely different listeners. Sony is the better all-rounder for noise cancellation and call quality; Sennheiser is the audiophile pick with double the battery life.

    Sources analyzed: Reddit (r/headphones) What Hi-Fi RecordingNow TechRadar · How we score →

    Quick Verdict

    Choose Sony WH-1000XM5 if…

    ANC is your top priority (commute, office, flights), you want the best call quality, and the most polished app experience.

    Choose Sennheiser Momentum 4 if…

    What Each Does Best

    Sony WH-1000XM5

    • Best-in-class noise cancellation — Both What Hi-Fi and TechRadar specifically credit the XM5 with class-leading ANC, edging out the Momentum 4 in most environments. Source: What Hi-Fi, TechRadar
    • Superior call quality — the XM5's 4-mic beamforming system outperforms the Momentum 4 for voice clarity in noisy environments. Source: What Hi-Fi
    • Better controls and app — Sony's Headphones Connect app and intuitive touch controls are consistently praised across reviewers for being more polished than Sennheiser's Smart Control app. Source: TechRadar, What Hi-Fi
    • More balanced default sound for general listening — while audiophiles prefer the Momentum 4, casual listeners often find the XM5 more "fun" and energetic out of the box. Source: RecordingNow direct A/B
    • Lighter weight (249g vs 293g) — meaningful for long sessions. Source: spec comparison

    Sennheiser Momentum 4

    • Audiophile-preferred sound signature — RecordingNow's audiophile A/B test specifically called out the Momentum 4 as having "more clarity, resolution, separation, wider and deeper soundstage, and more realistic timbre" than the XM5 — even after EQ. Source: RecordingNow audiophile review
    • 60-hour battery life with ANC on — exactly double the Sony's 30 hours. Genuinely transformative for travel. Source: What Hi-Fi, Sennheiser spec
    • More neutral, detailed sound — What Hi-Fi rated the sound as "crisp, detailed, and unbeatable" with neutral character vs Sony's bass-heavy default tuning. Source: What Hi-Fi, TechRadar
    • aptX Adaptive support — higher-quality Bluetooth codec for compatible Android sources (the XM5 only has LDAC). Source: TechRadar codec comparison
    • Genuinely competitive ANC — while the XM5 edges out in absolute terms, the Momentum 4 is still excellent and most listeners won't notice the difference outside of A/B testing. Source: TechRadar

    The Real Trade-offs

    Sony WH-1000XM5 downsides

    • Hinge durability — Reddit reports of XM5 hinges cracking. Source: r/headphones
    • Sound signature is bass-heavy by default — needs EQ for audiophile listening; even after EQ, RecordingNow audiophiles found the Momentum 4 still ahead on pure sound quality. Source: RecordingNow, HeadphonesAddict
    • 30-hour battery looks "weedy" next to Momentum 4 — TechRadar's specific phrasing. Source: TechRadar

    Sennheiser Momentum 4 downsides

    • Doesn't fold for travel — lies flat but doesn't fold compactly. Same issue as XM5 actually. Source: TechRadar
    • Less stylish, more utilitarian design — reviewers describe the look as "not the most stylish." Subjective but consistent feedback. Source: TechRadar
    • App less polished than Sony's — Sennheiser Smart Control works but feels older and less intuitive than Sony's. Source: What Hi-Fi
    • Heavier (293g vs 249g) — noticeable for longer sessions. Source: Spec comparison

    Our Recommendation

    This is one of the cleanest splits in flagship headphones. If you care about sound quality above all else and travel often, the Sennheiser Momentum 4 is the audiophile-recommended pick — double the battery and meaningfully better sound for serious listening. If you need the best noise cancellation, call quality, and an app you'll actually enjoy using daily, the Sony WH-1000XM5 is the right buy. Don't try to optimize both — these headphones are clearly built for different priorities.
