    Sony WH-1000XM5 vs Bose QuietComfort Ultra

    Two flagship noise-cancelling headphones at the same price tier, optimized for different things. Here's what owners and reviewers actually report across both — including the trade-offs that don't make it into spec sheets.

    Sources analyzed: Reddit (r/headphones) Tom's Guide What Hi-Fi Amazon.ca verified buyers · How we score →

    Quick Verdict

    Choose Sony WH-1000XM5 if…

    you value longer battery life (30 vs 24 hours with ANC), the best call quality in class, and slightly more detailed sound. Also runs cheaper.

    Choose Bose QC Ultra if…

    you wear headphones for 6+ hour stretches and prioritize all-day comfort, or want spatial audio for video and gaming.

    What Each Does Best

    Sony WH-1000XM5

    • Class-leading call quality — Tom's Guide and What Hi-Fi consistently rank the XM5 above the Bose for voice calls; the 4-mic beamforming + Precise Voice Pickup combination outperforms Bose's call processing in noisy environments. Source: Tom's Guide, What Hi-Fi
    • 30-hour battery (ANC on) — six hours longer than the Bose Ultra's 24-hour rating, which matters for travel and multi-day use without charging. Source: What Hi-Fi, manufacturer-verified
    • More detailed, agile sound signature — reviewers describe it as "punchy and precise" with better bass clarity, though some audiophiles find Sony's tuning slightly bass-heavy by default. Source: What Hi-Fi, RecordingNow
    • Multipoint Bluetooth + LDAC support — connects to two devices simultaneously and supports hi-res Bluetooth audio. Source: Sony spec, Tom's Guide

    Bose QC Ultra

    • All-day comfort — Tom's Guide's month-long head-to-head specifically called out the QC Ultra as more comfortable for extended wear; Bose padding distributes pressure better than the XM5 headband. Source: Tom's Guide direct comparison
    • Slightly stronger ANC overall — What Hi-Fi rated the QC Ultra's noise cancellation marginally above the XM5, particularly in higher-frequency cabin and office environments. Source: What Hi-Fi, Tom's Guide
    • Richer, fuller-bodied sound — especially in the bass; reviewers describe it as more "musical" for casual listening, though less detailed than the Sony. Source: What Hi-Fi
    • Immersive Audio (spatial audio) — Bose's CustomTune spatial audio adapts to ear shape and works well for movies and gaming, where Sony has nothing equivalent. Source: Tom's Guide, Bose spec

    The Real Trade-offs

    Sony WH-1000XM5 downsides

    • Hinge durability concerns — multiple Reddit reports of XM5 hinges cracking from minor drops or stress; reviewers and owners flag the new design as less robust than the XM4's. Source: r/headphones, GSMArena owner comments, RecordingNow
    • Ears get hot during longer sessions — Tom's Guide and Amazon.ca buyers consistently mention warm ear cups after 2+ hours of use. Source: Tom's Guide, Amazon.ca verified buyers
    • Doesn't fold compactly — the XM5 only folds flat, where the previous XM4 folded into a more carry-friendly shape. Source: What Hi-Fi

    Bose QC Ultra downsides

    • Higher price, shorter battery — typically $30-$100 CAD more than the XM5 with six fewer hours of battery life. Source: Tom's Guide, What Hi-Fi pricing
    • Immersive Audio is inconsistent — What Hi-Fi described Bose's spatial audio as "hit and miss," with some content sounding artificial. Source: What Hi-Fi
    • Earpad break-in period — multiple owners on GSMArena and Reddit report the earpads feel stiff out of the box and need a few weeks of regular use to soften. Source: GSMArena owner discussion

    Our Recommendation

    For most buyers, the Sony WH-1000XM5 is the better all-around choice — equivalent or better in nearly every category, with longer battery and a lower price. The Bose QuietComfort Ultra earns its premium specifically if you wear headphones for 6+ hour stretches and find ear comfort more important than the last 5-10% of every spec, or if spatial audio for movies and gaming matters to your use case.
