#1 Best Gas Mower — Honda HRX217VKA

Why it ranked

The Honda HRX217VKA earns an 8.9 ClearPick score — the highest of any mower in this guide — primarily on longevity and Canadian-climate durability. The GCV200 engine is one of the most reliable small engines documented by owners, with reports of 15–20 year service lifespans. The NeXite composite deck resists the rust and corrosion that kills steel decks after Canadian winters. The hydrostatic drive and Versamow simultaneous mulch-and-bag system are genuine differentiators no battery mower in Canada matches.

What owners love

Owner longevity reports are the most striking data point in any mower category: "Bought it in 2009 and it still runs like new. Fifteen years and three oil changes." The rust-free NeXite deck earns specific Canadian callouts: "NeXite deck looks exactly the same as the day I bought it, six Canadian winters later. Steel decks on my neighbours' mowers are all rusted out." Honda engine reliability is consistently cited: "Honda engine starts on the first or second pull every single time, even after sitting all winter with stabilizer." Versamow earns enthusiastic praise from lawn-care-focused buyers: "The Versamow feature is legitimately different. I run it at 50/50 mulch-bag ratio and my lawn has never looked better."

Most common complaint

Price is the dominant complaint — approximately $1,099 CAD upfront is a significant investment versus battery alternatives: "Over $1000 is a lot to spend on a lawn mower. I understand it lasts forever but the upfront cost compared to a $400 battery mower is hard to justify." Fuel stabilizer discipline in fall is a recurring Canadian-specific complaint: "Forgot fuel stabilizer last fall and spent two hours in May troubleshooting the carb." Weight (approximately 97 lb) draws mention from owners who manage it in and out of storage.

Canadian winter storage note

Canadian owners consistently report that fall fuel stabilizer treatment is non-negotiable. Ethanol-blended gasoline (E10, which is standard at Canadian pumps) gums carburetors after winter storage without stabilizer. Add fuel stabilizer every fall before storing — this is the most common Canadian-specific maintenance failure reported in owner reviews.

Best for

Gas-comfortable buyers who want a mower they will not replace in their lifetime, owners with medium to large lots (over 600 sq m), and buyers who value the unique Versamow simultaneous mulch-bag feature.

Canadian price: Approximately $1,099 CAD. Available at Home Depot Canada and Canadian Tire.

What to Look For When Buying a Lawn Mower in Canada

Battery runtime for Canadian lot sizes

Canadian urban and suburban lots average approximately 500 square metres. Most battery mowers in this guide handle that on a single charge in normal conditions. Spring wet grass significantly increases motor load and reduces runtime — budget for approximately 20–30% shorter battery life when cutting overgrown or wet spring grass.

Self-propelled for Canadian hills

Many Canadian residential properties have sloped lots — particularly in Vancouver, Victoria, Halifax, and older Toronto neighbourhoods. Push mowers become exhausting on any slope above approximately 10 degrees. Self-propelled drive is a genuine quality-of-life upgrade for hilly properties. The Honda HRX217VKA's hydrostatic drive and the EGO LM2156SP's variable-speed drive both handle moderate slopes without wrestling.

Mulching vs. bagging for Canadian lawns

Mulching returns nitrogen to the soil and is recommended for most Canadian lawns through the growing season. Bagging is preferable for fall leaf cleanup and for lawns with thatch buildup. The Honda HRX217VKA's Versamow system is the only mower in this guide that simultaneously mulches and bags in variable ratios — a unique capability for owners who want both.

Spring wet grass performance

Canadian spring often means tall, wet grass that has been growing under snow melt for weeks. This is the hardest mowing condition for battery mowers — overgrown wet grass bogs motors on underpowered platforms. The EGO LM2156SP's 56V brushless motor handles this condition; the Greenworks 40V 17" struggles in heavy spring grass. Gas mowers are generally most capable in this condition.

Electric vs. gas for provincial regulations

Ontario's Drive Clean regulations and Quebec's emissions standards are increasingly restricting gas-powered lawn equipment in residential areas. Several Ontario municipalities restrict gas mower use during summer smog days. Battery and electric mowers are exempt from these restrictions and operate year-round without compliance concerns. For buyers in regulated municipalities, battery mowers eliminate future regulatory risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I store a battery mower in a cold Canadian garage all winter?

The mower body can stay in an unheated garage, but lithium-ion batteries should be stored indoors at room temperature. Storing batteries at sustained sub-zero temperatures degrades cell capacity and reduces total battery lifespan. Most Canadian owners bring the battery indoors each fall and store the mower body in the garage without issues. EGO recommends storing at 50% charge for long-term storage.

Is a robot mower practical for a typical Canadian suburban lot?

Robot mowers work well on open lots without complex obstacle patterns. The Mammotion LUBA 3 handles up to 1,500 sq m — adequate for most Canadian suburban properties. Challenges include lots with many trees, flower beds, children's equipment, and irregular shapes that require complex zone mapping. Setup takes 30–90 minutes for a typical lot. Once running, robot mowers genuinely operate autonomously and owners report high satisfaction on suitable lots.

Which mower handles the best in wet spring Canadian grass?

The Honda HRX217VKA handles wet, thick spring grass best — the GCV200 engine has more torque headroom than battery alternatives, and the Versamow system manages clipping volume effectively. Among battery mowers, the EGO LM2156SP's 56V brushless motor handles spring conditions acceptably; the Greenworks 40V 17" struggles. All robot mowers should not operate in heavy rain or immediately after — their navigation and blade systems perform best on dry or lightly damp grass.

Is EGO available in Canada?

Yes. EGO Power+ products are available at Home Depot Canada, Canadian Tire, and Amazon.ca. The 56V battery platform is fully supported in Canada with replacement batteries, chargers, and accessories available through major Canadian retailers. EGO's warranty service is available through Canadian retailer service centres.

What are the blade replacement costs for Canadian buyers?

Honda HRX217VKA replacement blades (the MicroCut twin-blade system) are available at Honda dealers and Canadian Tire — expect approximately $40–60 CAD per blade set. EGO LM2156SP replacement blades are available on Amazon.ca and at Home Depot Canada for approximately $30–50 CAD. Mammotion LUBA 3 replacement blades are available through Amazon.ca direct — availability is generally strong but the brand's Canadian distribution is newer and less established than Honda or EGO.
