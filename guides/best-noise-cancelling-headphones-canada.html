Best Noise-Cancelling Headphones in Canada: Ranked by Real Owner Sentiment (2025)

Best noise-cancelling headphones in Canada — ranked by owner data across 20,000+ reviews. What commuters, remote workers, and travellers actually buy and keep.

Published 2026-06-21 by ClearPick

We ranked the top noise-cancelling headphones available in Canada by ClearPick Score — a composite built from verified Canadian owner reviews. Seven models are included across three price tiers: premium ($549–$599 CAD), mid-range ($349–$379 CAD), and budget ($79 CAD). Rankings reflect ANC performance, real-world comfort across long sessions, and the complaints that actually show up in owner data — not lab specs.

1. Bose QuietComfort Ultra — Best ANC Headphones in Canada

  • Why it ranked first: The Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones scores 8.8 overall with a comfort subscore of 9.0 — the highest comfort rating in this guide. ANC scores 9.5, matching the Sony WH-1000XM6. Where the Bose wins is wearability: owners consistently report 6–8 hour wear sessions without ear fatigue, something the XM6 gets mixed reviews on.
  • What owners love: "Set it and forget it" comfort appears in roughly 50% of positive reviews — the oval-shaped ear cups work well for owners with larger ears. ANC performance on transit and in offices is the second most-cited praise. Immersive Audio spatial mode gets genuine positive mentions from music listeners.
  • Most common complaint: Battery life scores only 6.5 — at approximately 24 hours with ANC on, the Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones is the worst battery life of any headphone in this guide. Appears in roughly 35% of negative reviews. For commuters who charge daily, not a dealbreaker; for travellers on long hauls, it matters.
  • Best for: Remote workers, open-plan office employees, and commuters who wear headphones 4+ hours daily and prioritize not having sore ears at the end of the day.
  • Canadian price: ~$599 CAD on Amazon.ca

3. Sony WH-1000XM6 — Best ANC Technology

  • Why it ranked third: The Sony WH-1000XM6 scores 8.7 with the highest ANC subscore (9.5, tied with the Bose) and sound quality subscore of 9.0. It ranks third because the comfort subscore drops to 7.5 — noticeably lower than the Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones and Sony WH-1000XM4. For buyers who prioritize silence above all else and don't wear headphones for 6+ hours straight, it's the top technical performer.
  • What owners love: ANC in loud environments — plane cabins, busy transit, open offices — is the top praise category appearing in roughly 45% of positive reviews. The processor upgrade from the Sony WH-1000XM4 handles wind noise and mixed environments better. Sound quality at 9.0 is praised by listeners who want both ANC and audio fidelity.
  • Most common complaint: Comfort is the dominant negative — headband pressure on longer sessions appears in roughly 30% of critical reviews. The Sony WH-1000XM6 is noticeably heavier than the Sony WH-1000XM4 and owners with larger heads report pressure points after 3–4 hours.
  • Best for: Frequent flyers, commuters with short-to-medium sessions (under 4 hours), and listeners who want the best ANC technology currently available at a non-audiophile price point.
  • Canadian price: ~$549 CAD on Amazon.ca

What to Look For

ANC Depth vs. Comfort Trade-Off

The deepest ANC headphones — the Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones and Sony WH-1000XM6 — handle this differently. Bose prioritizes long-session comfort; Sony prioritizes maximum silence. For commuters wearing headphones 6+ hours daily, the Bose comfort advantage is consistently reported in owner data. For frequent flyers on shorter hauls, the XM6's silence advantage wins.

Canadian Pricing vs. US

Most of these headphones cost $50–$100 more on Amazon.ca than Amazon.com. The Sony WH-1000XM4 at approximately $373.60 CAD is the best illustration of strong Canadian pricing — it offers near-XM6-tier ANC for substantially less than the current-generation model.

Multipoint Bluetooth for Remote Workers

If you switch between a work laptop and phone regularly, multipoint Bluetooth matters. The Sony WH-1000XM6, Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones, and Sennheiser MOMENTUM 4 Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones all support reliable multipoint. The Sony WH-1000XM4's multipoint implementation is notably less reliable per owner data — a real consideration for remote workers.

Battery Life for Your Use Case

Daily commuters who charge at night can live with 24-hour battery (Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones). Remote workers who forget to charge need 30+ hours. Travellers on multi-day trips without reliable power access should look seriously at the Sennheiser MOMENTUM 4 Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones' 60-hour battery — no other option in this guide comes close.

Ecosystem Lock-In

The Apple AirPods Max Wireless Over-Ear Headphones with USB-C Charging is the clearest ecosystem play in this guide. Seamless iPhone/Mac switching and spatial audio integration are real features AirPods Max owners value — but they don't translate to Android or Windows users. If you're considering switching phones, avoid locking into Apple headphones.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which noise-cancelling headphones are best for a Canadian commute?

The Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones and Sony WH-1000XM4 are the top picks for daily transit commuters. The Bose wins on all-day comfort; the XM4 wins on price. Both handle subway and bus noise well per owner data.

Is the Sony XM6 worth the upgrade from the XM4 in Canada?

For most buyers, no. The Sony WH-1000XM6 costs approximately $175 more than the Sony WH-1000XM4 in Canada. The ANC improvement is incremental; the comfort regression (heavier, more pressure) is real per owner data. If you're on the XM4 and happy, there's no compelling upgrade case at current Canadian pricing.

Do any of these headphones have good call quality for work-from-home?

The Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones and Sony WH-1000XM6 score best for call quality based on owner reports. Both use multi-mic beamforming that handles background noise during calls. The Sennheiser MOMENTUM 4 Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones is specifically called out by remote workers for clear voice pickup.

How much more do these headphones cost in Canada vs. the US?

Expect 15–25% more at current exchange rates. The Bose QuietComfort Ultra Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones retails around USD $329 (US); it's approximately $599 CAD. Amazon.ca is generally the best Canadian price point — better than Best Buy Canada on most models.

Can I get ANC headphones under $100 in Canada that actually work?

Yes — the Anker Soundcore Q45 Adaptive Active Noise Cancelling Headphones at approximately $79 CAD scores 8.1 overall with genuine ANC performance. It won't match the Bose or Sony, but it works well for office environments and mid-level transit noise. A legitimate entry point for first-time ANC buyers in Canada.
