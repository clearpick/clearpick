What to Look For

Capacity for Your Household Size

This is the most important variable. A 4-quart basket serves 1–2 people per batch. A 6-quart basket serves 3–5. The most common regret in air fryer owner reviews is buying too small and cooking in batches daily — which defeats the convenience promise. Canadian households averaging 2.9 people sit right at the 4–6 quart crossover point.

Cleaning Effort

All three picks have dishwasher-safe baskets. The real differentiator is what else accumulates grease — the Instant Vortex Plus 6-Quart Air Fryer with ClearCook Window's viewing window is the only component that cannot be dishwashered and becomes permanently greasy with regular use. Owners who use their air fryer daily rate cleaning ease as their second most important factor in long-term satisfaction.

"I use it every single day and the basket goes in the dishwasher every night. Takes thirty seconds to wipe the interior. The only component I struggle with is the window — and after six months I've basically accepted it will never look clean. Everything else about this machine is exactly what I wanted."

Amazon reviewer (Instant Vortex Plus 6qt owner)

Preheat Time

Most air fryers in this category take 3–5 minutes to preheat. The Cosori Pro Gen 2 is marketed as preheat-free. In practice, owners who preheat report more consistent results on denser foods (chicken thighs, thick-cut fries). For reheating leftovers or cooking thin foods, preheat time is less important.

Counter Space

Large-capacity air fryers take up significant counter space. The Instant Vortex Plus 6-Quart Air Fryer with ClearCook Window is considerably larger than the Ninja AF101 footprint. If counter space is tight — particularly in older Canadian homes and apartments — capacity needs to be weighed against where the unit will live permanently.

Noise Level

Air fryers are not quiet appliances — they run roughly 50–65 dB during operation, similar to a moderate conversation. Apartment owners who have noise-sensitive neighbours mention this. None of the picks in this price range are meaningfully quieter than the others.

FAQ

What foods do owners say air fryers do best?

Frozen foods, chicken wings, French fries, reheating leftovers (pizza, previously fried foods), and roasted vegetables get the most consistent praise across owner reviews. Foods owners report disappointment with: thick cuts of red meat (uneven results), baked goods (require accessories), and anything with wet batter (slides off before cooking).

Which air fryer is best for a Canadian apartment?

The Ninja AF101's compact footprint is the most cited advantage for apartment kitchens — under 13 inches wide, it fits on most smaller counters. If you need more capacity and have the counter space, the Instant Vortex Plus 6-Quart Air Fryer with ClearCook Window is the better family-size option.

Are these air fryers available at Canadian Tire and Walmart Canada?

Yes. The Ninja AF101 and Instant Vortex Plus 6-Quart Air Fryer with ClearCook Window are regularly stocked at Canadian Tire, Walmart Canada, and Best Buy Canada in addition to Amazon.ca. Buying in-store lets you return more easily if the size doesn't work for your kitchen. Prices are generally within 5–10% of Amazon.ca.

Is it worth spending more than $150 on an air fryer?

For standalone air frying, no — the Instant Vortex Plus 6-Quart Air Fryer with ClearCook Window and Ninja AF101 cover the actual cooking needs of most Canadian households. The exception is if you want a multi-function appliance: the Cuisinart TOA-65 Digital AirFryer Toaster Oven with Convection at $249 CAD adds full toaster oven capability and earns its price if it replaces two appliances.
