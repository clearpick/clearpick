Road Running Shoes

1. HOKA Clifton 9 Men's Road Running Shoe — 9.2/10

Why it ranked here: Highest ClearPick score in the running shoe category. Stack height (9.5), plush cushioning (9.5), and transition smoothness (9.5) are all top-tier — the Clifton 9 is the benchmark for maximalist cushioning in a lightweight package. Reddit discussions on r/running consistently place the Clifton series as the top recommendation for new runners and those returning after injury because the cushioning absorbs impact on hard surfaces without the instability that some maximalist shoes create.

What owners love: The most common praise is the first-run experience — owners describe an immediate comfort sensation that doesn't require a break-in period. Pushing pairs to 700+ km before replacement is reported by multiple long-term owners. Running on hard Canadian pavement and frozen sidewalks is mentioned as where the Clifton 9 outperforms lighter, less-cushioned alternatives.

Most common complaint: Durability subscore of 8.5 (slightly below other subscores) — the foam midsole compresses noticeably around 600–700 km for heavy runners. The wide toe box is praised by most but occasionally mentioned as too loose by narrow-footed runners.

Best for: Runners logging high weekly mileage, recovery runs, anyone with knee or joint sensitivity who runs on hard surfaces, beginner runners in Canadian urban environments.

Canadian price: ~$189 CAD on Amazon.ca and at Running Room locations

2. Brooks Ghost 15 Men's Road Running Shoe — 9.1/10

Why it ranked here: Second-highest score and the most versatile all-rounder in the catalog. Cushioning (9.5), transition (9.5), durability (9.0), and versatility (9.0) all score high — this is the shoe owners most frequently describe as "does everything well without excelling in any single direction." In Canadian running communities, the Brooks Ghost is the shoe most frequently recommended for someone who wants one shoe for road running, casual hiking, and gym use.

What owners love: Value subscore of 8.5 — owners consistently mention getting more than expected relative to price. The shoe holds up well across all four Canadian seasons when used for road and light trail. Fit consistency across multiple pairs is praised — runners who buy a second pair find it fits identically to the first.

Most common complaint: Less maximalist cushioning than the Clifton 9 — runners who specifically want the plush feel find the Ghost 15 firmer. Runs slightly narrow for wide-footed runners; wide width option is available but sometimes harder to find in Canadian sizes.

Best for: Everyday training, runners who want one shoe for multiple surfaces, Canadian runners who train year-round including gym sessions in winter months.

Canadian price: ~$159 CAD on Amazon.ca, SportChek, and Running Room

"Three years running in Ghost models — 14s, then 15s. The 15 is slightly better cushioned and the outsole grips wet pavement better, which matters in Vancouver from October through April. I've tried Hoka and Asics but keep coming back to the Ghost for how consistent the fit is across every pair."

What to Look For

Road vs. trail in Canada

Most Canadian runners need a shoe that handles both pavement and light trail. The Brooks Ghost 15 is the top recommendation for mixed-surface use where the trail is packed dirt or gravel. For proper trail running with mud, roots, and wet rock, dedicated trail shoes like the Salomon X Ultra 4 GTX provide grip that road shoes cannot match.

Winter running in Canada

Canadian winter pavement — ice, compacted snow, and salt-treated concrete — changes shoe requirements significantly. Owners who run outdoors through Canadian winters most frequently recommend shoes with either: (a) a wider outsole for stability on slippery surfaces, or (b) compatibility with YakTrax or similar ice cleats that attach to the outsole. Neither the Clifton 9 nor the Ghost 15 is designed for ice — icy conditions require YakTrax or dedicated winter running shoes.

Canadian retailers vs. Amazon.ca

Running Room Canada offers gait analysis and in-person fitting that Amazon cannot. For runners uncertain about their pronation or arch type, visiting a Running Room for a fitting — then purchasing at the best price online — is the approach most recommended in Canadian running communities. Running Room's return policy for footwear is more flexible than Amazon.ca's standard policy.

Km tracking for Canadian runners

Canadian runners track distance in kilometres, not miles. The HOKA Clifton 9's effective lifespan of 600–700 km translates to roughly 375–435 miles. Most running shoes from major brands recommend replacement at 500–800 km of road running — the exact number varies by runner weight, surface, and gait. Garmin and Apple Watch both track lifetime km on shoes when configured through their respective apps.

Wide width availability in Canada

Wide widths are available in both the HOKA Clifton 9 and Brooks Ghost 15 but may be harder to find in stock at Canadian retailers. Amazon.ca and Running Room's online store typically carry more size and width combinations than brick-and-mortar locations. Ordering two widths and returning the wrong fit is a common approach Canadian buyers use.

FAQ

Is HOKA or Brooks better for Canadian runners?

Based on owner data: HOKA Clifton 9 is better if cushioning and joint comfort on hard surfaces are the priority. Brooks Ghost 15 is better if you want a versatile shoe that performs across multiple surfaces and conditions. Both are available at Running Room Canada, which allows side-by-side trying.

Do running shoes perform differently in cold Canadian weather?

Yes. Foam midsoles stiffen in cold temperatures, which reduces cushioning and responsiveness until the shoe warms up — typically the first 10–15 minutes of a cold-weather run. This is a known characteristic across all foam-based running shoes. Owner reviews from Canadian runners occasionally note this as a warm-up consideration in winter runs below -10 degrees Celsius.

Can I use trail shoes for road running in Canada?

Trail shoes like the Salomon X Ultra 4 GTX are designed for off-road grip and will wear faster on pavement than road shoes. The Contagrip outsole loses tread noticeably on hard pavement surfaces. For mixed road-and-trail use, a road shoe with moderate outsole grip (Brooks Ghost 15) handles more surface variety than a dedicated trail shoe.

Where do Canadian runners buy running shoes?

Running Room is the dominant specialty running retailer in Canada with locations in most provinces. SportChek carries major brands but with less specialty depth. Amazon.ca has the widest selection and typically the most competitive pricing, with free returns on most footwear. MEC is the top option for trail and outdoor footwear in Canada, including the Salomon X Ultra 4 GTX.
