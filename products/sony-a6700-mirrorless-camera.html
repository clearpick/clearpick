    ✅ What Works

    • 4K/120fps 10-bit is the defining spec for this price tier — enabling true 4x slow motion at full UHD resolution with log colour profile for grading. No other APS-C camera in this price range offers this combination.
    • AI subject recognition is the most comprehensive AF system in APS-C — tracking for people (eye, face, body), dogs, cats, birds, insects, cars, motorcycles, trains, and planes. The system maintains lock even when subjects partially occlude or cross behind obstacles.
    • Sony E-mount is the largest mirrorless lens ecosystem — hundreds of native Sony lenses plus full AF support from Sigma, Tamron, Zeiss, and others. The upgrade path from kit lens to premium glass is more affordable and more varied than Canon RF or Nikon Z.
    • 5-stop IBIS combined with lens IS produces 6-7 stop effective stabilization in compatible combinations — sufficient for handheld video at walking pace without a gimbal.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • No built-in flash — the a6700 omits the pop-up flash included in older Sony APS-C models. An external flash or trigger is required for fill flash work.
    • Menu system has improved but remains complex — Sony's menus have a learning curve that Canon's more intuitive system avoids. New Sony users should expect an adaptation period.
    • UHS-II SD slot is present but not UHS-II write speed in all formats — most 4K recording modes use UHS-I equivalent write speeds; UHS-II primarily benefits burst buffer depth.
    • $1,699 is premium for APS-C — at this price, full-frame alternatives like the Sony ZV-E1 are worth comparing for video-focused buyers.
