    ✅ What Works

    • The four-position orbital action selector is the most useful feature for versatility. Position 0 delivers a straight reciprocating cut for fine finish work in laminates and veneers; positions 1 through 3 add progressively more aggressive oval stroke motion that removes material faster in softwood framing and rough cuts. Most users find position 2 the ideal general-purpose setting.
    • Tool-free blade change via the front-mounted release lever handles all T-shank blades — the industry standard — without a wrench or hex key. Blade swaps take under ten seconds, which matters when you're switching between a fine-tooth blade for plywood and a bimetal blade for cutting metal conduit.
    • The cast aluminum shoe (base plate) distributes the cutting force more evenly than plastic shoes on budget jigsaws, reducing vibration at the cut surface and improving line-following accuracy. The 45-degree bevel capacity with the bevel lock gives clean angled cuts for trim carpentry and countertop scribing.
    • Variable speed from 0 to 3,000 SPM means you can dial in the right speed for the material — slow for acrylic and tile (with appropriate blades), fast for softwood. The 3,000 SPM top speed is brisk enough to cut 3/4-inch pine cleanly in a single smooth pass.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • This is a tool-only product — no battery or charger included. You need a 20V MAX battery to use it. If you don't already own 20V MAX tools, budget an additional $60–$100 CAD for a compatible battery and charger, or buy the DCK combo kit that includes batteries.
    • At 5.5 inches maximum depth of cut in wood, the DCS331B is suited for dimensional lumber and sheet goods up to about 2× material. For cutting through doubled 2×4 framing or stacked lumber, a reciprocating saw or a deeper-cutting jigsaw is the better tool.
    • Blade drift — where the cut wanders off a straight line — is a function of blade quality and technique more than the tool itself. Using quality T-shank blades (Bosch T101B for fine cuts, T144D for fast cuts) and letting the tool lead without forcing makes a substantial difference in cut quality.
    • The orbital action setting affects blade life: position 3 cuts faster but wears blades more quickly, and leaves a rougher edge. For finish cuts in hardwood or laminate, position 0 or 1 extends blade life significantly and produces a cleaner edge.
