    ✅ What Works

    • The Magic Clip's fade blade is what separates it from every other consumer hair clipper. The extended taper lever allows you to adjust the blade exposure in fine increments, enabling smooth fade transitions from skin to short to longer lengths that are otherwise only achievable with scissors and professional technique. This is the primary reason barbers worldwide use this specific clipper — the fade capability is genuine professional-grade functionality.
    • Corded/cordless versatility is the Magic Clip's practical differentiator from many competitors. 90 minutes of cordless runtime is sufficient for full professional haircuts and typical home use sessions; when the battery runs low, you plug in and continue without interruption. This eliminates the frustrating situation where a battery-only clipper dies mid-cut.
    • The rotary motor maintains consistent blade speed under load — pulling through thick or dense hair doesn't slow the cut the way a magnetic motor does. For home users cutting their own or family members' hair in various growth densities, motor consistency means cleaner lines and fewer passes over the same section.
    • Eight colored guide combs covering 1/8" to 1" in graduated lengths cover every standard cut length without additional purchases. The color coding makes grabbing the right comb intuitive — after a few sessions you reach for the right color automatically without reading size markings.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Magic Clip is a professional-grade tool that produces professional results only with practice. Fading specifically requires learning technique — clipper-over-comb, scooping motions, blending in transitions — that takes time to develop. Expect 5–10 cuts before your fades look clean. Watch tutorials before your first use.
    • Blade oil maintenance is required for optimal performance and longevity. Wahl recommends oiling the blade every 3–5 uses. Clipper oil is inexpensive ($5–10 CAD for a bottle that lasts years) but skipping it reduces blade performance and life. This is a fundamental maintenance step, not optional.
    • The blades can be zeroed-gapped (adjusted so the upper and lower blades align flush) for an even closer cut at skin level. Zero-gapping produces excellent skin-fade results but the adjustment requires some mechanical comfort and Wahl provides instructions. Many users never do this and still get excellent results.
    • Wahl's guide combs are magnetic-clip style, which hold securely but can pop off if you're aggressive with angle changes. First-time users occasionally lose a guide mid-cut. Press the comb against the clipper body firmly when changing angles to prevent this.
