    ✅ What Works

    • 40MP sensor with Fujifilm's colour science produces JPEG files so well-rendered that many owners skip editing entirely — 20 film simulations including Reala Ace and Classic Negative are the main reason photographers buy this camera over technically superior alternatives.
    • IBIS is the transformative upgrade over the X100V — owners report sharp handheld shots at 1/4s to 1/8s. Low-light street photography without a tripod or flash is now realistic.
    • Leaf shutter produces near-silent operation — discreet for street photography and documentary work where a loud mechanical shutter would change subject behaviour.
    • Fixed 35mm f/2 lens forces deliberate composition that many owners say improves their photography — the camera does one thing and does it exceptionally.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Availability is the biggest real-world problem — this camera launched February 2024 and has remained chronically backordered at Canadian dealers (Henry's, Vistek, Best Buy Canada, Visions Electronics) more than two years later. Used prices run inflated. The page is worth building; check stock before linking.
    • Lens is the weakness in the weather sealing claim — the body is sealed but the lens is not. Full dust and moisture protection requires the optional adapter and filter, which adds bulk and a step before shooting in rain.
    • IBIS added approximately 48g over the X100V — the camera weighs 521g and is no longer pocketable in jeans. A coat pocket or small bag is required.
    • UHS-I memory card slot is a spec disappointment at 40MP — high-speed burst shooting and 6.2K video will buffer noticeably on slower cards.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The X100VI is the compact camera that other compact cameras are judged against — 40MP, IBIS, 20 film simulations, and a 35mm f/2 lens in a body that still fits a coat pocket. Autofocus is the one area where it genuinely shows its age, and the supply situation in Canada remains frustrating two years in. If you can find one at MSRP, buy it without hesitation.
