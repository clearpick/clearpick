    ✅ What Works

    • 4K front resolution provides the license plate legibility and detail at distance that 1080p cameras miss—the difference is meaningful in an insurance claim or collision dispute where plate capture at 30 meters is the critical evidence frame
    • Sony STARVIS 2 sensor performance in low-light and night conditions is genuinely superior to standard CMOS sensors—headlights, streetlights, and high-contrast night conditions that cause overexposure on standard dash cams render correctly on the U1000
    • The dual-channel front+rear configuration is included in the box (not a separate purchase) and covers both the collision evidence from front and the tailgating/rear-end evidence from behind—comprehensive accident documentation in one package
    • Thinkware's parking mode with the included hardwire kit provides 24-hour vehicle monitoring for hit-and-run events in parking lots—relevant in urban Canadian environments where unattended parking damage is common

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • MicroSD card is not included and 4K front + 1080p rear recording fills storage faster than standard cams; a 128–256GB endurance-rated microSD card ($30–$60) is required and should be added to the purchase budget
    • 4K resolution means larger file sizes—the camera will overwrite older footage more quickly per GB of storage than a 1080p alternative; 128GB at 4K dual channel provides approximately 6–8 hours of loop-before-overwrite recording
    • The hardwire kit (needed for parking mode) requires connection to the vehicle's fuse box; if you're not comfortable with DIY wiring, a professional install adds $60–$100 to the total cost

    Hardwire kit install can be complex
