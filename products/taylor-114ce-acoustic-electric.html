    ✅ What Works

    • Taylor Expression System 2 electronics are the best under-saddle pickup system at this price — the ES2 uses an innovative behind-saddle pickup placement that captures more of the acoustic resonance than standard under-saddle systems, producing a plugged-in sound that retains more acoustic character than typical piezo pickups.
    • Grand Auditorium body shape balances projection and comfort — the GA body is Taylor's most versatile design: larger than a concert or OM for more projection, but narrower and more comfortable than a dreadnought for extended seated playing. It works for strumming and fingerpicking.
    • Taylor's neck profile and setup is consistently player-oriented — Taylor guitars leave the factory with low, comfortable action and a straight, even neck. More than any other major acoustic guitar brand, Taylor's out-of-box playability is consistently high.
    • Cutaway allows access to the upper frets — the single Venetian cutaway allows the picking hand to access frets above the 12th position for lead playing, making the 114ce appropriate for melody-focused playing styles.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Layered (not solid) walnut back and sides — only the top is solid. The layered walnut back and sides are engineered wood construction, which is humidity-stable but resonates less freely than solid wood back and sides. This is the standard trade-off at $1,099.
    • Grand Auditorium projects less than a dreadnought for acoustic-only use — the GA body shape has a more balanced, controlled projection rather than the booming volume of a dreadnought. For loud unplugged playing, a dreadnought projects more. For amplified or studio use, the GA's controlled response is advantageous.
    • ES2 requires a 9V battery — the Taylor Expression System 2 is battery-powered. A fresh 9V provides 70-100 hours of use, and the battery is accessed through a compartment on the end block. Replace before performances.
    • The included hardshell case is a genuine value-add — Taylor includes a padded hardshell case (normally $100-150 separately). Factor this into cost comparisons with guitars sold without cases.

    Common complaints

    Layered walnut back and sides limits…

    Layered walnut back and sides limits resonance versus all-solid alternatives — the back and sides are engineered wood, reducing the acoustic resonance that all-solid guitars achieve. Players upgrading from the Seagull S6's solid back and sides may notice the difference.

    Source: Reddit (r/acousticguitar)

    GA body projects less than dreadnought…

    GA body projects less than dreadnought for unplugged acoustic-only use — the Grand Auditorium produces less volume than a dreadnought body for players who play primarily acoustic without amplification.

    Source: Amazon.ca reviewer

    ES2 battery compartment access awkward…

    ES2 battery compartment access awkward mid-gig — the 9V battery replacement requires a small screwdriver or coin and is slightly fiddly to access during a performance. Change the battery before any gig.

    Source: Reddit (r/acoustic)
