    ✅ What Works

    • Twenty hours of battery life is the Charge 5's defining feature — it genuinely lasts all-day outdoor use, from morning paddleboarding to an evening campfire, without needing a recharge. The Bose SoundLink Flex and Sony XB23 don't come close to this endurance.
    • The built-in powerbank function is a smart feature that other portable speakers overlook — the USB-A port outputs enough power to charge a phone while the speaker is playing. At a festival, beach, or camping trip, having a speaker that also keeps your phone alive is practically useful.
    • IP67 waterproofing plus the JBL PartyBoost multilink system means you can chain multiple JBL speakers together for bigger sound at outdoor events — up to two speakers in stereo mode or more in party mode. The ecosystem build-out is more accessible than Sonos at this price point.
    • Sound signature is JBL's characteristic punchy, bass-forward tuning — great for outdoor environments where you need projection and bottom-end to carry over background noise. The two passive radiators on each end add noticeable bass presence that competing single-driver speakers can't match.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Charge 5 weighs nearly a kilogram — noticeably heavier than the Bose SoundLink Flex (590g) or JBL Flip 6 (550g). The extra weight comes from the larger battery and is a real consideration for hikers and backpackers counting every gram.
    • JBL's bass-forward tuning is great outdoors but can sound thick or muddy indoors for acoustic music, podcasts, or voice content. The EQ in the JBL app allows adjustment but the default tuning is clearly optimized for outdoor party scenarios.
    • The Charge 5 doesn't float — despite being fully waterproof, it sinks. Buyers planning pool or lake use should be careful about drops into deep water, unlike the Bose SoundLink Flex which floats.
    • USB-C charging is fast but the speaker itself only charges devices via the USB-A port — you cannot charge the Charge 5 via USB-A. The charging direction is one-way out, which catches some buyers off guard when they try to charge it from a USB-A power bank.
