    ✅ What Works

    • Concert size (vs. soprano) provides more fret access and wider spacing for adult hands — concert ukuleles have a longer scale length and wider neck than soprano ukes, making chord shapes easier for players with average or larger adult hands. Most guitar players transitioning to ukulele prefer concert or tenor size.
    • Aquila Nylgut strings are the industry-standard premium uke strings — the KA-C ships with Aquila Nylgut, which most uke players purchase and install separately on budget ukuleles that come with inferior plastic strings. Getting them factory-installed at $99 is a value advantage.
    • Mahogany top, back, and sides produce warm, balanced uke tone — the all-mahogany construction gives the KA-C a warm tonal character with good sustain. It sounds full and resonant for a beginner instrument.
    • Kala brand is the most trusted in the ukulele segment — Kala ukuleles are played and recommended by professional ukulele players and teachers. The brand credibility means better resale value, better community support, and confidence in quality control.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Ukuleles require significant tuning when new — new Nylgut strings take several days to break in and will go out of tune frequently for the first week of playing. This is normal for nylon-type strings and not a defect. Tune frequently at first.
    • Concert is not the smallest size — if you're buying for a child under age 8, consider a soprano ukulele. For teenagers and adults, concert is the right choice.
    • No tuner or bag included at the base price — a clip-on tuner ($15-20 CAD) is essential for keeping any ukulele in tune. The KA-C doesn't include one.
    • Not electro-acoustic — the KA-C has no built-in pickup. For performing at open mics or amplified settings, a separate clip-on pickup is needed or the KA-CG (with pickup) is the appropriate upgrade.
