    ✅ What Works

    • At 5.3 inches front-to-back and 2.0 lbs, the DCF787 is one of the most compact 20V MAX impact drivers DEWALT makes. The reduced length fits into tight spots — inside cabinet frames, between joists, and in cramped mechanical rooms — where a standard-length impact driver would wedge against an obstruction before the bit contacts the fastener.
    • 1,650 in-lbs of max torque handles deck screws, structural lag bolts up to 3/8-inch, and fastening through multiple layers of material without bogging. At this torque rating you will strip a screw head before you'll overload the tool, so consistent depth control comes from technique and clutch use rather than power limitation.
    • Variable speed trigger combined with 2,800 RPM and 3,400 IPM gives useful dynamic range — you can ease into a fastener at low speed for positioning, then accelerate into high speed for driving. The three LED brightness modes (off, low, medium) illuminate a work area rather than casting a hot spot, which reduces glare in enclosed spaces.
    • The 20V MAX 1.3Ah battery included is small but the platform compatibility is the value — this same battery works across all DEWALT 20V MAX tools. Upgrading to a 2.0Ah or 5.0Ah battery triples or quintuples runtime without any other changes.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The 1.3Ah battery included with the kit is genuinely small — roughly 20-30 minutes of intermittent fastening before recharge. Consider this a starter battery that gets you working immediately, not the battery you'll use on real projects. A 20V MAX 2.0Ah or 4.0Ah battery ($35–$60 CAD) is the realistic companion for full-day use.
    • Impact drivers use a hexagonal 1/4-inch chuck — they accept 1/4-inch hex shank bits only. Standard round-shank drill bits require a 1/4-inch hex adapter. If your primary need is drilling holes (not driving fasteners), a drill/driver with a standard chuck is more practical; the DCF787 excels at screwdriving, not drilling.
    • Like all impact drivers, this tool is loud — around 85 dB at the operator's ear during impact cycles. Hearing protection is recommended for extended use. The impacts also transmit noticeable vibration to the wrist and forearm, which becomes relevant for anyone with hand or wrist conditions.
    • The 3-LED lights are located behind the chuck, which can cast the shadow of the chuck body onto the fastener. For precision work in dark spaces, repositioning the light angle by rotating the tool slightly is more effective than relying on the LEDs directly.
