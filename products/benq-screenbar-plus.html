    ✅ What Works

    • The asymmetric optical design is the key differentiator — light is angled downward onto the desk only, never reflecting back into the camera or creating a glare hotspot on the monitor screen. This is a genuine engineering achievement that cheaper LED desk lamps don't replicate.
    • The wireless dial controller on the desk is the feature that separates the ScreenBar Plus from the standard ScreenBar. Instead of reaching up to touch controls on the bar, you twist and press a small dial on your desk to adjust brightness and color temperature instantly.
    • The built-in ambient light sensor automatically adjusts the light output as room conditions change — useful during work sessions that span from daytime to evening without requiring any manual adjustment.
    • Setup takes under two minutes: clip the bar to the top of any monitor up to about 1.5 inches thick, plug the USB-A cable into the back of the monitor or PC, and the wireless dial connects automatically. No app, no Bluetooth pairing, no wall outlet required.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At $199 CAD, this is a significant spend on a desk light. The standard BenQ ScreenBar (without the wireless dial) costs around $109 and has the same optical design. The $90 premium buys you wireless desk control only — worth it if you frequently adjust brightness, less essential if you set it once and forget it.
    • The light bar clips to the top bezel of your monitor. Thin bezels on modern monitors work fine, but frameless or very curved monitors may not have enough surface for a secure clip.
    • The wireless dial uses a USB-A receiver that plugs into the bar itself. The desk controller doesn't need any extra dongles, but it's worth noting this is a proprietary wireless protocol, not Bluetooth.
    • Color accuracy of the light itself is rated at CRI 95+, which is good for close work like reading or drawing — colors appear natural rather than washed or shifted under the light.
