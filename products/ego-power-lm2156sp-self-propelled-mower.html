    ✅ What Works

    • Variable-speed self-propel adjusts naturally to your walking pace — a dial on the handle lets you dial in your preferred speed rather than fighting a fixed drive rate. Owners report it works well on gentle slopes without requiring extra effort.
    • The 56V brushless motor handles thick, overgrown grass without bogging that plagues weaker battery mowers. Multiple owners note it chews through a week's growth after rain without slowing.
    • 7.5Ah battery covers roughly a third of an acre per charge — adequate for most urban and suburban Canadian lots. The 56V platform is shared with EGO's blowers, trimmers, and chainsaws, so one battery ecosystem covers the full yard tool set.
    • Weather-sealed construction handles Canadian spring conditions — light rain and morning dew won't sideline the mower. The steel deck and reinforced housing feel closer to gas-mower durability than typical battery options.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Battery and charger inclusion varies by listing — some Amazon listings are tool-only, others are full kits. Confirm the kit version before ordering, as the 7.5Ah battery alone runs $150–$200 CAD.
    • At $849 CAD, this is priced above comparable gas self-propelled mowers. The total-cost advantage only materializes over several seasons of gas and maintenance savings.
    • The 21" deck is standard for residential mowers but not best-in-class — some competitors offer 22". On lots over half an acre, extra passes add up noticeably.
    • Charge time is roughly 90 minutes with the standard charger. EGO's rapid charger (sold separately) brings this down significantly but adds cost.
