    ✅ What Works

    • The Coanda effect — where fast-moving air creates suction that draws objects toward a surface — is what makes the Airwrap fundamentally different from every other curl tool. Hair wraps around the barrel via airflow rather than being clamped at extreme heat, which means you're styling with 150°C instead of the 230°C+ most curling irons require. Less heat means less damage over years of use.
    • Replacing three separate tools (hair dryer, curling iron, straightening brush) with one device that lives in a case has real daily-life convenience value. The attachment swap takes 5 seconds, and the storage case keeps everything organized — this matters if you travel with your styling tools.
    • The long barrel (1.2 inch) creates loose, natural-looking waves that most curling irons struggle to replicate without leaving a kink at the end. The Airwrap's results consistently look more effortless than clip-curling-iron results because there's no tool mark where the clamp holds the hair.
    • Results on fine and medium hair are exceptional — the Coanda effect works best when it can attract lighter hair easily. Users with fine hair report salon-quality curls that last all day. Thick hair works too but may need longer hold time on the barrel.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The $699 CAD price is the most significant drawback. For that money you could buy a professional curling iron, a salon-quality blow dryer, and a quality straightening brush separately. The Airwrap justifies its cost for daily users who value low heat damage, convenience, and storage — but it's a luxury purchase.
    • The learning curve is real, especially for beginners. Hair needs to be damp (not wet, not dry) for optimal results on the curl barrels. The technique of getting hair to wrap consistently takes most people 2–3 weeks of practice before it becomes intuitive.
    • Hair wrapping direction requires different barrel orientation for left-side and right-side curls. The Complete Long set includes two versions of each barrel size for both directions, which eliminates fumbling — but you need to consciously use the right barrel for the section you're styling.
    • Very thick or coarse hair may not get consistent Coanda attraction and could require working in smaller sections than finer hair types. Dyson's results are best documented on fine to medium hair; thick hair users should watch unsponsored reviews for their specific hair type before purchasing.
