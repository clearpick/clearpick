    ✅ What Works

    • 90MB/s write speed is sufficient for 4K/60fps on virtually every mirrorless camera and action camera on the market — it meets or exceeds the minimum speed requirements for continuous 4K video recording without dropped frames.
    • 200MB/s read speed dramatically reduces the time to offload large video files to a computer — transferring 64GB of 4K footage takes under 6 minutes versus over 20 minutes with a slow reader and standard UHS-I cards.
    • SanDisk's reliability track record is the strongest in consumer SD cards — in extended use reports across photography forums, the Extreme PRO line consistently shows lower failure rates than competing brands at the same price point.
    • RescuePRO Deluxe data recovery software is included with purchase — a 1-year subscription that can recover accidentally deleted photos and videos, providing meaningful protection for professional shoots.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • UHS-I (not UHS-II) — cameras with UHS-II card slots (like the Sony a6700 and Fujifilm X-T5) can use faster UHS-II cards for even quicker offloading and deeper burst buffers. The Extreme PRO is limited to UHS-I speeds in those cameras.
    • 128GB holds approximately 2 hours of 4K/30fps footage at typical bitrates — heavy video shooters working on multi-day projects should carry multiple cards or a portable SSD.
    • Counterfeit risk on discount sites — SanDisk is among the most counterfeited SD card brands. Purchase from Amazon.ca directly or an authorized retailer, not third-party marketplace sellers, to avoid fake cards.
    • Write speed is guaranteed at 90MB/s, not 200MB/s — the 200MB/s is sequential read speed. Some marketing conflates these numbers; the write speed is what matters for in-camera recording.
