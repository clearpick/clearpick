    ✅ What Works

    • ghd's fixed 185°C temperature is not a limitation — it's a deliberate design decision backed by their own research showing 185°C is the optimal zone for effective styling with minimal protein damage to hair. Most users crank variable-temp irons to 230°C+ out of habit; the ghd forces the optimal temperature and produces equivalent results with substantially less damage over time.
    • Predictive ultra-zone technology monitors temperature 250 times per second and adjusts heating across the plates 100 times per second. The practical result is that plates maintain 185°C precisely throughout a styling session, even when you're taking section after section continuously — most irons lose 20–30°C between sections and spike on contact to compensate.
    • The ceramic floating plates glide through hair without the micro-snagging that lower-quality plates produce. Combined with the precise temperature, you typically need fewer passes than with a variable-temperature iron — which is the actual driver of reduced heat damage.
    • Auto sleep mode that triggers after 30 minutes of inactivity is a meaningful safety feature for a consistently-on iron. Unlike irons that simply turn off, the ghd's auto-sleep preserves the timer so you can resume if you pick it back up within the window.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The fixed temperature system is a deal-breaker for some users, particularly those with very fine or bleached hair who prefer to style at 150°C or lower. Ghd's position is that 185°C is optimal for all hair types, but stylists working with severely damaged hair sometimes want lower. The Dyson Airwrap is the better choice for ultra-low-heat styling.
    • At $299 CAD, this is more expensive than the BaBylissPRO Nano Titanium at $129 CAD. The ghd's advantage is in temperature precision and predictive technology, not in raw performance metrics like heat speed. For users with fine or color-treated hair who want the most gentle effective straightener, the price difference is justified.
    • The 2.7m cord is impressively long and universal voltage (100–240V) which makes it travel-friendly internationally. The cord is attached with a swivel joint but the joint can stiffen over years of use.
    • Ghd uses proprietary ceramic plates that the brand claims last longer and maintain smoother surfaces than competitor ceramics. Replacement plates are not user-serviceable; if the plates are damaged the iron needs to be repaired or replaced.
