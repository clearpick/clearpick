    ✅ What Works

    • Weber's Flavorizer bars are the key differentiator of the Spirit II over competing gas grills. Angled metal bars sit above the burners and catch drippings, which vaporize and infuse smoke flavor into the food above. This is the primary reason Weber gas grills taste different from competitors with open-flame designs — the Flavorizer system genuinely produces better flavor from a gas grill than you'd otherwise get.
    • Even heat across the cooking grate is a Weber hallmark and the Spirit II E-310 delivers it reliably. Three burners at even spacing produce a cooking surface where the center and edges finish food at the same rate. If you've cooked on grills with uneven heat where the middle always burns and the edges barely cook, the difference a well-designed burner layout makes is immediately obvious.
    • The Spirit II E-310 is the compact version in Weber's lineup, making it the right choice for condo decks, small backyards, or anyone who wants a premium grill that doesn't dominate the outdoor space. At 52 inches wide with tables folded out, it fits in spaces where a 4-burner grill simply wouldn't.
    • Weber's warranty and service infrastructure in Canada is unmatched. The 10-year warranty on all parts except normal wear items, combined with Weber's broad presence at Canadian retailers and easy parts availability, means this is a grill you can buy knowing you'll be supported for a decade. Weber's customer service reputation is consistently among the best in the category.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At 30,000 BTU and 424 sq in primary cooking area, the Spirit II E-310 is sized for a family of three to four people. It's not a grill for cooking for a crowd — if you regularly entertain eight or more people, look at the Weber Genesis E-325s or Genesis E-435 for more capacity. The E-310's strength is being the right-sized premium grill for everyday family use.
    • 30,000 BTU is on the lower end for a three-burner gas grill. The Spirit II heats evenly but preheat time is slightly longer than higher-BTU competitors, and searing temperatures are adequate rather than exceptional. For burgers, chicken, and vegetables it's perfectly fine — for high-heat steak searing, a GrillGrates upgrade noticeably improves results.
    • The folding side tables are convenient for smaller spaces but not as sturdy as fixed tables on competing grills at this price. They work well but feel slightly light under heavy use — putting a full bag of groceries on them is not recommended.
    • Note that this listing (ASIN B01G3IT71U) and the existing site product 'weber-spirit-ii-e310-gas-grill' are the same product line. The Spirit II E-310 has been sold under slightly different model numbers and configurations on Amazon.ca over time — verify the specific configuration (cart vs. without, color) matches what you want before purchasing.

    Common complaints

    Lower BTU than comparable competitors

    At 30,000 BTU, the Spirit II produces less raw heat than Broil King Baron 420 (40,000 BTU) or Napoleon Prestige 500 (66,000 BTU). It's enough for most home grilling but buyers who want the fastest preheat and highest sear temperatures should look at higher-BTU options.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    No side burner on E-310

    Unlike the competing Broil King Baron 420, the Spirit II E-310 does not include a side burner. Buyers who want to boil corn, heat sauces, or use a side burner need to step up to the Weber Genesis line.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Side tables feel lightweight

    The folding side tables are useful for the compact footprint but feel flimsy compared to the rest of the grill's build quality. They're fine for a plate and tongs but not for heavy items.

    Source: Reddit

    Price has increased since launch

    Several long-time Weber buyers note the Spirit II now costs more than it did at launch for the same product. At $699 CAD, the value comparison against Broil King and Napoleon becomes tighter.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
    ClearPick Verdict

    The Weber Spirit II E-310 is the best compact premium gas grill for Canadian buyers who want Weber's trademark even heat, Flavorizer flavor system, and 10-year warranty in a right-sized package for everyday family cooking. It's not the highest-BTU or largest-capacity grill at this price — it competes with the Broil King Baron 420 on quality and the value is closer than it used to be. But for buyers who prioritize Weber's brand reputation, service network, and even-cooking performance over raw power and cooking area, the Spirit II E-310 earns its price and then some.
