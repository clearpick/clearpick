    ✅ What Works

    • Compatible with the broad RYOBI 40V battery ecosystem — the same batteries work across RYOBI 40V trimmers, blowers, hedge trimmers, and snow blowers. Existing RYOBI 40V users get immediate tool compatibility.
    • The HP Brushless motor runs noticeably cooler and more efficiently than standard brushed motors, translating to longer runtime per charge and longer motor lifespan over years of use.
    • 21" deck covers typical Canadian residential lots (3,000–5,000 sq ft) without the extra-passes frustration of 17" budget mowers. Variable-speed self-propel adjusts to your walking pace on both flat and gently sloped terrain.
    • Quieter than any gas mower — a genuine quality-of-life difference for early morning mowing in Canadian suburbs where noise bylaws are common.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • RYOBI sells two incompatible battery systems: 40V and 18V ONE+. They share no batteries. Many buyers with RYOBI 18V tools assume compatibility — it does not exist. Confirm 40V before buying.
    • Battery inclusion varies by listing — tool-only listings are common. Confirm the kit version; 40V 6Ah batteries cost $100–$130 CAD separately.
    • The 40V platform delivers slightly less power than EGO's 56V for very thick, overgrown, or wet grass. Weekly cutting keeps it within comfortable range; occasional users may struggle after missed weeks.
    • Customer service reports are mixed — warranty issues have been resolved slowly for some Canadian buyers. Keep purchase receipts.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The RYOBI 40V HP Brushless 21" sits between the budget Greenworks and the premium EGO — broader ecosystem than Greenworks, more affordable than EGO, and more cutting power than entry-level options. The RYOBI 40V/18V battery confusion is a recurring real problem worth knowing going in. For buyers already in the RYOBI 40V ecosystem or looking for a solid mid-range self-propelled mower, it earns its place. For buyers starting fresh, the EGO's 56V ecosystem and superior thick-grass performance justify the ~$150 premium.
