    ✅ What Works

    • The 6-microphone beamforming array is the core differentiator for business use — it focuses on your voice and suppresses background noise before the audio leaves the headset. Call participants consistently report hearing you clearly even when you're working in a noisy open-plan office or home environment.
    • The dual-device connectivity is practically useful: the USB-A dongle connects to your work PC while Bluetooth pairs to your phone simultaneously. You can switch between a Teams call on your computer and a mobile call without touching any pairing settings.
    • Battery life of 37 hours means a full work week on a single charge for most users — a Monday morning charge carries through Friday without worry. The USB-C charging cable and the option to use the headset while charging add additional flexibility.
    • Microsoft Teams certification goes beyond cosmetic branding — the Teams button on the headset launches Teams, answers and ends calls, and shows Teams call status via the LED indicator. For organizations standardized on Teams, this integration saves time across dozens of calls per week.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Evolve2 65 is a professional business headset, not a consumer audio headset. Music listening quality is decent but not the priority — Jabra optimizes for voice clarity, not bass or soundstage. If you want a headset that's equally good for music and calls, look at Sony or Bose.
    • At $299 CAD, this competes against the Poly Voyager Focus 2 and Logitech Zone Wireless. All three are excellent; the Evolve2 65 generally leads on call clarity and beamforming performance, while the Poly and Logitech options offer slightly more comfortable ear cushions for very long sessions.
    • The USB-A dongle is required for the primary low-latency wireless connection. Bluetooth is available as a secondary connection but introduces slightly more latency than the dongle. For calls, always use the dongle.
    • The flexible boom microphone arm folds up when not in use but is more visible than in-ear boom designs. In video calls, the arm is visible on camera — some users prefer a less conspicuous setup. The mic audio quality is noticeably better than any mic-embedded-in-headband design.

    Common complaints

    Heavy for all-day wear…

    The over-ear design with the boom arm gets noticeable after 4-5 hours of continuous wear. Not painful but I take it off between calls to give my ears a break.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Expensive compared to consumer options…

    You can get consumer wireless headsets with ANC for $100–150. The Evolve2 65 costs twice as much — justified by call clarity if your job is on calls all day, but hard to justify for occasional use.

    Source: Reddit

    USB dongle occupies a port…

    The USB-A dongle is always plugged in and taking up a port. On a laptop with limited USB ports this is a real consideration, especially if you also use a USB-A wireless mouse.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Teams app integration requires software…

    The Teams button requires the Jabra Direct software to be installed and running for full functionality. The headset works without it but you lose some Teams-specific features.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
