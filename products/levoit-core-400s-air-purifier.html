    ✅ What Works

    • Real built-in AQI sensor with true auto mode is the differentiator at this price — the PM2.5 laser sensor detects particle concentration and automatically adjusts fan speed within seconds of a pollution event (cooking, candle, guests arriving). This is genuinely reactive, not just a scheduled timer.
    • VeSync app is the best air purifier app in this price tier — polished, reliable, with real-time air quality graphs, historical data, scheduling, and energy usage. Compared to Winix's no-app approach or Dyson's data-hungry cloud system, Levoit's implementation is balanced.
    • 403 sq ft coverage at 2 ACH covers standard Canadian living rooms — for allergy and asthma sufferers, running at a higher fan speed to achieve 4-5 ACH reduces allergen exposure more significantly.
    • 24 dBA sleep mode matches the Core 300 — quiet enough for bedroom use despite being a full living-room unit, allowing 24/7 continuous operation including overnight in adjacent rooms.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • 403 sq ft at 2 ACH is the maximum coverage claim — HEPA effectiveness depends on air changes per hour. For allergy control, 4-5 ACH is recommended, reducing effective coverage to ~200 sq ft. Know your room size.
    • Filter replacement at ~$40/yr — slightly more expensive than the Core 300 filter due to larger filter surface area. Still less than Dyson or Blueair.
    • The Coway AP-1512HH has comparable CADR at a lower price without app features — buyers who don't want or need app control should compare the Coway, which often costs less and has a superior long-term reliability record.
    • AQI sensor is a laser PM2.5 sensor — excellent for detecting particulate pollution (dust, pollen, smoke, pet dander) but doesn't measure gases, VOCs, or CO2. For comprehensive sensing, Dyson's multi-sensor system is more comprehensive.
