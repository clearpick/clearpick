    ✅ What Works

    • Cats are instinctively attracted to moving water over standing water — an evolutionary preference for fresh over stagnant sources. The Catit Flower Fountain's three flow modes satisfy this instinct, and owners who switched from standing bowls consistently report their cats drinking more water daily. Increased hydration is particularly important for cats prone to urinary issues or kidney problems, and many vets now recommend fountains as a standard preventive measure.
    • Three flow modes accommodate different cats' preferences — the flower spray is visually interesting and encourages paw-dipping interaction, the calm stream suits cats that prefer a less dramatic flow, and the bubbling center caters to cats that like drinking from the lowest surface. Multi-cat households often find that different cats gravitate to different modes.
    • The triple-action carbon filter removes hair, debris, and chlorine taste from tap water, producing a meaningfully fresher-tasting result than an unconditioned standing bowl. The filter replacement every 30 days is straightforward and the replacement filters are widely available and inexpensive.
    • All parts disassemble without tools and are dishwasher safe on the top rack, which is the deciding factor for long-term hygiene. Fountains that can't go in the dishwasher develop biofilm in unreachable crevices that eventually creates health concerns. Dishwasher compatibility makes weekly cleaning a genuine habit rather than a frustrating manual process.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The pump produces a low hum that's audible in a quiet room. Most owners adapt to it quickly, but placement in a bedroom may disturb light sleepers — the utility room, kitchen, or living room are better locations than a bedroom.
    • The pump and flower head require cleaning every 1–2 weeks to prevent limescale buildup, which gradually restricts flow and increases pump noise. Owners in hard-water areas (much of Ontario and Alberta) will need to clean more frequently than the manual suggests.
    • At 3 liters the capacity is appropriate for 1–2 cats. Multi-cat households of 3+ cats will need to refill more frequently than once a week, or consider a larger capacity fountain.
    • Replacement filters are the recurring cost — typically $10–15 CAD for a pack of 3. At one filter per month the annual filter cost runs $40–60 CAD, which should factor into the true cost of ownership.
