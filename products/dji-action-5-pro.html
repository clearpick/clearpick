    ✅ What Works

    • 4K/120fps is the defining spec — DJI is the first action camera to achieve this, enabling true 4x slow motion at full UHD resolution. At 4K/60fps and below, the footage quality is noticeably sharper and cleaner than GoPro HERO13.
    • 47-minute battery life is nearly double the GoPro HERO13 in matched conditions — in real-world activity shooting, this means completing a full mountain bike run, surf session, or ski run without battery anxiety.
    • 1/1.3-inch sensor produces cleaner high-ISO footage than any previous action camera — low-light performance at ISO 3200 is usable where GoPro footage at the same setting shows obvious noise.
    • HorizonSteady 360° lock keeps the horizon level even during full barrel rolls — genuinely impressive for motorsports, skiing, and mountain biking where the camera physically rotates.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • More expensive than GoPro HERO13 — the Action 5 Pro commands a $150+ premium over the GoPro, which may not be justified for casual users who don't need 4K/120fps.
    • DJI accessories are not compatible with GoPro mounts — if you have an existing GoPro mount ecosystem (helmets, bikes, surfboards), you'll need to rebuy adapters or DJI-specific mounts.
    • Slightly larger than GoPro due to the bigger sensor — still wearable and mountable, but the size difference is noticeable in tight helmet mounts.
    • The touchscreen can be hard to operate with gloves or wet hands — DJI's voice commands work as a workaround in cold or wet conditions.
