    ✅ What Works

    • The Duo Plus adds sterilize and sous vide functions over the base Duo model — sterilize is genuinely useful for home canning, baby bottles, and fermentation equipment; sous vide works at lower wattages than a dedicated circulator but produces good results for thick cuts cooked at precise temperatures.
    • The blue LCD display with a progress indicator shows remaining cook time and pressure status at a glance — clearer than the base Duo's simpler readout. For owners who walk in and out of the kitchen mid-cook, knowing where the pressure cycle is at any moment is useful.
    • Fifteen smart programs cover the most common pressure cooking tasks with tested default times — ribs, beans, chili, rice, oatmeal all have one-touch programs that work well without manual time adjustment. The community cookbook ecosystem for Instant Pot Duo Plus is enormous.
    • The advanced steam-release valve is a design improvement over the original Duo — it has a more controlled release rate that reduces sputtering of thick liquids (stews, soups) during quick release, which was a minor but real mess issue on earlier Instant Pot models.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At $149 vs $129 for the base Duo, the upgrade to Duo Plus is primarily for the sterilize and sous vide functions. If you don't plan to can, sterilize equipment, or cook sous vide, the base Duo at $20 less does everything else identically.
    • Sous vide performance in the Instant Pot is limited — the pot can only maintain temperatures within ±2°C of the target and doesn't circulate water. For steak and fish where 0.5°C matters, a dedicated circulator is significantly more precise. For chicken breasts and pork, it works fine.
    • The sealing ring odor issue that affects the base Duo is identical here — curry and garlic aromas absorb into the silicone ring and transfer to subsequent cooks. Buying a second dedicated ring for sweet/dairy cooks is recommended.
    • The silicone mitt included with the Duo Plus is genuinely useful for handling the inner pot after pressure cooking — the stainless steel pot retains heat and the mitt allows safe handling without a towel. Many users overlook this accessory entirely.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Instant Pot Duo Plus is a strong upgrade from the base Duo for home canners and anyone who wants sous vide functionality in a pot they already own. For everyone else, the $20 premium over the base Duo buys functions most users won't use regularly. As a standalone product it's an excellent 9-in-1 multi-cooker at a fair price — the pressure cooking, slow cooking, and sauté functions are identical in performance to the base model, and the LCD display is a genuine improvement.
