    ✅ What Works

    • Milwaukee's QUIK-LOK tool-free accessory change system is the fastest accessory swap mechanism in the oscillating tool category. A single lever releases and clamps the accessory without tools, alignment keys, or hex bolts — you swap a blade in under 5 seconds, versus 30-60 seconds on older threaded or hex systems. For tasks requiring frequent blade switches (grout removal → undercutting → sanding), this compounds into significant time savings.
    • Accessory compatibility with STARLOCK and OIS standards means the 2626-20 accepts accessories from Fein, Bosch, Makita, and nearly any other OIS-compatible brand alongside Milwaukee's own catalog. This breadth matters because blade specialty is where oscillating tools live or die — having access to the full market of carbide grout blades, specialty scraper blades, and fine-tooth wood blades rather than one brand's lineup is a genuine advantage.
    • Variable speed from 11,000 to 18,000 OPM gives appropriate range for oscillating tool tasks. The 11,000 OPM low end handles delicate sanding in veneer and finish trimming without generating heat that would scorch thin material; the 18,000 OPM top end drives through drywall and rough framing cuts efficiently.
    • The M18 battery ecosystem is Milwaukee's flagship platform — over 250 tools share the same batteries. The 2626-20 accepts any M18 battery from 1.5Ah to the flagship 12.0Ah REDLITHIUM HD, giving precise runtime control depending on job size. Existing M18 owners add this tool without any new battery investment.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The 2626-20 uses a brushed motor, not brushless. Milwaukee makes a brushless M18 FUEL multi-tool (2836-20) at roughly $100 CAD more that offers longer runtime and better performance under sustained heavy loads. For occasional use, the brushed 2626-20 is fine; for daily professional use like grout removal across full bathrooms, the FUEL version's brushless advantage becomes tangible.
    • This is a tool-only purchase — no battery or charger included. M18 batteries range from $60 (2.0Ah) to $200 (12.0Ah) on Amazon.ca. If you don't own M18 tools, the battery investment is substantial; a kit version of the 2626-20 with a battery included is sometimes available and worth checking before buying tool-only.
    • Oscillating tools are inherently loud and vibratory. The 2626-20 generates similar vibration to all cordless oscillating tools at this power level — wear hearing protection for sessions over 20 minutes and take breaks to avoid hand-arm vibration fatigue. Anti-vibration gloves reduce transmission further.
    • The 3.2° oscillating angle is slightly wider than the DEWALT DCS356 (3.0°). In practice, this means marginally faster material removal but also a slightly coarser cut finish. The difference is small and matters only in very fine finish work where surface quality at the cut edge is critical.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Milwaukee 2626-20 is a capable M18 oscillating tool for homeowners and occasional-use professionals who want Milwaukee's QUIK-LOK accessory system and broad OIS compatibility without the FUEL brushless premium. The tool-only format suits existing M18 users perfectly. Buyers doing heavy daily use — full bathroom tile removal, extended grout work — should budget for the M18 FUEL 2836-20 instead, where the brushless motor's sustained performance justifies the extra cost.
