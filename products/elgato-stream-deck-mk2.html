    ✅ What Works

    • The 15 LCD keys displaying custom icons make it immediately obvious what each button does — no memorizing which blank key triggers which macro. This visual clarity is what separates the Stream Deck from keyboard shortcut memorization, making it genuinely faster to use over time.
    • The Stream Deck plugin ecosystem covers over 200 applications and services with dedicated integrations. OBS scene switching, Spotify track skipping, Zoom mute toggle, and Premiere playback controls each get dedicated plugins that respond instantly without switching windows.
    • Nested pages mean 15 physical keys can control hundreds of actions — you can set any key to open a folder page of related actions. A single 'Recording' key can expand to a page of 15 recording-specific controls, then close back to your main profile.
    • For remote workers, the Stream Deck handles video call management with one-touch mute, camera toggle, raise hand, and application switching — faster than keyboard shortcuts and visible from across the desk without squinting at key labels.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Stream Deck requires the Elgato Stream Deck desktop app running in the background to function. The app is well-made and low on resources, but it's worth knowing this is not a standalone hardware button pad — it needs the software.
    • At $229 CAD, this is a productivity tool aimed at professionals and power users, not a casual purchase. The value proposition improves significantly the more applications and repetitive workflows you actively use.
    • The detachable USB-C cable is an improvement over the original Stream Deck's fixed cable, but the cable exits from the bottom rather than the back — worth knowing for desk cable management planning.
    • Elgato releases the Stream Deck in multiple sizes: Mini (6 keys), MK.2 (15 keys), and XL (32 keys). The MK.2 is the right balance for most users. Start here and upgrade only if you run out of key real estate after several months of use.
