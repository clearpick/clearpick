    ✅ What Works

    • The 57° vertical angle addresses repetitive strain injury risk at the core — it keeps the forearm in a neutral handshake position rather than the flat pronated position of standard mice. For users with existing wrist or forearm discomfort, the switch to a vertical mouse often provides immediate relief.
    • The compact size targets users with small-to-medium hands who find the MX Vertical's large grip overwhelming. Most mainstream vertical mice assume large hands; the Lift's shorter height and tighter grip shape is a meaningful differentiator for the significant portion of users who find full-size ergonomic mice uncomfortable.
    • Dual connectivity — Logi Bolt USB receiver or Bluetooth 5.0 — covers both scenarios: a desktop without Bluetooth uses the receiver; a laptop with Bluetooth and limited USB ports uses wireless pairing without occupying a port.
    • Twenty-four months of battery life from a single AA battery makes the Lift essentially maintenance-free in practice. Most users won't change the battery more than once a year, and the low battery indicator gives advance warning before it dies.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Lift is designed for small-to-medium hands specifically. Users with hands larger than about 19cm may find the grip cramped, particularly with the pinky finger having limited support. The MX Vertical ($149) is the right choice for large-handed users who want a vertical mouse from Logitech.
    • The 4000 DPI maximum is adequate for standard office productivity at typical monitor resolutions, but falls short for high-DPI large-format monitors or users who want fine pixel-level control for design work. The Lift is a productivity mouse, not a precision tool.
    • The scroll wheel is a standard click-to-click wheel without the MagSpeed freewheel capability of the MX Master 3S. For navigating long documents, the Lift's scrolling is functional but not as fast.
    • The vertical design has an adjustment period — most users need 1-3 weeks before vertical mousing feels as natural as a standard mouse. Wrist position improvement is typically felt quickly; fine motor precision at the new angle takes longer to develop.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Logitech Lift is the best-value entry into ergonomic vertical mice, particularly for users with small-to-medium hands who find most vertical mice too large. The 57° angle and proper grip shape provide the forearm comfort benefit without the size or price of the MX Vertical, and the 24-month battery life with dual connectivity make it a low-maintenance daily driver. The two-to-three-week adjustment period is the only real barrier, and the wrist comfort payoff makes the transition worth it for most users.
