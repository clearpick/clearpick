    ✅ What Works

    • 24 dBA on sleep mode is the quietest air purifier we test — comparable to a very soft whisper or leaves rustling in a light breeze. This enables continuous overnight operation in bedrooms occupied by light sleepers without any sleep disruption.
    • Interchangeable filter options allow specializing the unit without buying a new purifier — switch to the Pet Allergy filter during allergy season, the Toxin Absorber filter during renovation, or the Smoke Stopper filter during wildfire season. Each filter is $25-35 CAD.
    • 360° air intake draws from all sides of the cylindrical body — more effective room coverage compared to single-intake competitors at the same size, because it captures particles from every direction rather than just in front.
    • Nightstand-sized footprint at 26cm tall — the compact cylinder fits on a bedside table, keeping it close to sleeping occupants where bedroom air quality improvement matters most.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • No auto mode or air quality sensing — the Core 300 (not the 300S WiFi version) requires manual speed selection. It can't detect air pollution events and ramp up automatically. If auto mode matters, look at the Levoit Core 400S or Coway AP-1512HH.
    • 219 sq ft is a bedroom-only unit — for a living room, den, or master suite over 300 sq ft, the Core 400S or Coway AP-1512HH provides better coverage.
    • Filter replacements at ~$30 every 6-8 months — annual filter cost is $45-60, lower than Blueair and Dyson but higher than Coway. The specialty filter options add cost if rotating between types.
    • LED display cannot be fully turned off on all models — the ambient glow from the touch panel may be visible in a dark bedroom. A piece of dark tape over the display solves this for some users.
