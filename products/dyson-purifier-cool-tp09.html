    ✅ What Works

    • 800 sq ft coverage in auto mode is genuine for open-plan living rooms and large bedrooms — the TP09 is Dyson's most capable standalone air purifier, with 360° HEPA filtration that draws air from all directions rather than just one side.
    • Sealed HEPA system prevents air bypassing the filter — Dyson's 360° intake design combined with a sealed filter housing captures 99.97% of 0.3-micron particles without the gaps found in typical single-intake purifiers.
    • Formaldehyde catalytic filter continuously destroys formaldehyde (from furniture, flooring, and building materials) without needing replacement — unlike activated carbon which saturates and needs replacement, the catalytic filter performs the same function indefinitely.
    • Auto mode with AI responds to real-time pollution events within seconds — during cooking, a visitor spraying hairspray, or external pollution ingress, the fan automatically ramps up and returns to baseline when the event passes.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • $799 is a significant premium with a high total cost of ownership — filter replacement at ~$70/yr means spending $1,149 over 5 years on this one device. A Coway AP-1512HH + Vornado 630 fan achieves comparable results in most rooms for ~$350 total.
    • Fan cooling efficiency is less than dedicated fans — the Air Multiplier design prioritizes air quality over raw airflow. In summer heat, a standalone tower fan moves more air per dollar.
    • MyDyson app data goes to Dyson servers — indoor environment data including VOC levels, temperature, and usage patterns is stored in Dyson's cloud infrastructure.
    • 42 dBA at lowest is louder than Coway and Levoit at their lowest settings — the purifier is not whisper-quiet in the way the Blueair 411i Max or Levoit Core 300 are.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Dyson TP09 is among the best air purifiers available, but it's an expensive way to get there. Sealed HEPA, 800 sq ft coverage, formaldehyde destruction, and real-time sensing are all genuine advantages. The total cost of ownership and the ongoing filter expense are real considerations. Buy it if Dyson's build quality and integrated design matter to you; choose a Coway + fan if you want maximum performance per dollar.
