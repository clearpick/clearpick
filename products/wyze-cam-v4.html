    ✅ What Works

    • 2.5K resolution at $39 CAD is extraordinary — most security cameras at this price offer 1080p, and several charge $100+ for QHD. The Wyze Cam v4's 2560×1440p image is noticeably sharper for identifying faces, license plates, and package details in playback.
    • A 32GB microSD card is included in the box, enabling continuous local recording with no subscription required. Most competitors at this price don't include the card — and many don't even have a microSD slot. With the included card and continuous recording enabled, you get days of 24/7 footage without spending a cent more.
    • 5GHz Wi-Fi support is a meaningful upgrade over the v3 and most competing cameras — a 5GHz connection delivers faster live view loading and more stable streaming in environments with congested 2.4GHz networks. It's the first Wyze camera to include dual-band support.
    • Color night vision in well-lit areas (porch lights, street lamps) delivers color detail that infrared-only cameras miss. During daylight hours, the wide 130° field of view captures more of an entryway or backyard than most competing cameras.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Color night vision requires ambient light — in true darkness, the camera falls back to infrared black-and-white. The color night vision marketing can create inflated expectations; it's better described as enhanced-light color mode rather than true low-light color capability.
    • The free Wyze app includes 14 days of rolling event-triggered cloud clips, which is generous. Full 24/7 cloud recording requires Wyze Cam Unlimited at $5.99/month USD. The included microSD card covers 24/7 continuous recording locally without a subscription.
    • Wyze has faced data security incidents in the past — a 2019 data exposure and a 2024 incident where camera feeds were briefly visible to wrong users. The company responded with fixes and security improvements, but privacy-sensitive buyers should weigh this history.
    • The camera is wired (USB-A to USB-C) with no battery option — it needs to be within reach of a power source or use a long USB cable run. Outdoor installation typically requires a weatherproof USB cable or an outdoor-rated power strip.

For budget-first security coverage of entryways, driveways, and common areas, it's hard to beat.
