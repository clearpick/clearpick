    ✅ What Works

    • The Honda GCV200 engine is one of the most reliable small engines ever made — owners routinely report 15–20 years of service with basic annual maintenance (oil change, air filter, spark plug). In Canadian climates where mowers sit all winter, Honda's reliability advantage over competing engines is significant.
    • The NeXite composite deck cannot dent, rust, or corrode — a direct answer to the chronic rust problem on steel decks after Canadian winters. Owners with 10+ year old HRX217s consistently report the deck still looks new.
    • Versamow is a genuine differentiator: the twin-blade MicroCut system can simultaneously mulch AND bag in variable ratios, letting you fine-tune how much clipping goes back into the lawn. No other mower at this price does this.
    • Hydrostatic drive gives infinite speed control — not a few fixed settings but a true throttle. On uneven or sloped terrain, fine speed control makes a meaningful difference in comfort and cut quality.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At $1,099 CAD, this is the most expensive mower in its class. The total cost of ownership over 15+ years is competitive, but the upfront hit is substantial.
    • Gasoline dependency means spring startup after winter storage requires a fresh fuel check. Ethanol-blended gas (E10) can gum up carburetors after a winter without fuel stabilizer — a real Canadian spring headache.
    • Annual oil changes are required and non-negotiable. The maintenance schedule is simple, but it's more than battery mowers ask for.
    • At ~97 lb it's heavier than any battery alternative — moving it in and out of storage, or up ramps into a garage, is more effort than lighter cordless options.
