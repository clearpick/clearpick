    ✅ What Works

    • The Brooks Ghost 15 has held the title of America's best-selling running shoe for years — not through marketing, but because it genuinely does the daily trainer job better than almost anything else at its price. The DNA LOFT v2 midsole foam is the key: it's plush without being unstable, responsive without being firm, and maintains its cushion properties through hundreds of miles. If the Ghost 14 was the reliable workhorse, the Ghost 15 adds a noticeable softness upgrade while keeping the same heel-to-toe transition geometry.
    • The segmented crash pad on the outsole is a technology that's been refined over 15 generations of Ghost, and it shows. Landing heel-first or midfoot, the shoe guides your foot through the stride without the abrupt thud you get in cheaper shoes or the unstable roll feeling in highly maximalist shoes. It's a genuinely smooth ride that makes long runs feel less punishing — particularly important at the 20–30km mark when tired legs become sloppy legs.
    • The 3D Fit Print upper wraps the foot rather than constraining it, which means the Ghost 15 fits a wide variety of foot shapes without the break-in period many performance shoes require. It's available in narrow, standard, wide, and extra-wide sizing — a width range that very few shoes at this price match — and the engineered mesh is breathable enough for summer running without sacrificing structure. The heel collar is well-padded without gripping so hard it causes Achilles irritation.
    • Durability is a legitimate strength. Most runners report 800–1,000+ km from a pair of Ghost 15s before meaningful midsole compression, which is above average for a daily trainer. At $159 CAD, the cost-per-kilometer works out favorably against premium options at $200+. Brooks also backs the shoe with a 90-day wear test return policy — you can run in them for three months and return them if they're not working.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Ghost 15 is built for neutral runners. It has no significant stability features — no guide rails, no medial post, no significant arch support. If you overpronate significantly, a podiatrist would likely recommend a stability shoe instead. Brooks makes the Adrenaline GTS for that purpose, which shares the Ghost's cushioning profile with added support.
    • At 279g per shoe, the Ghost 15 is a moderate-weight daily trainer. It's not a racing flat or a speed shoe — the Hyperion Max or a carbon-plated racer is what you want for tempo runs and races. The Ghost excels at easy to moderate effort runs where recovery and mileage accumulation matter most.
    • The 8mm heel-to-toe drop places the Ghost in the traditional trainer category. Runners who have transitioned to lower-drop shoes (4mm or less) may find the heel height feels elevated. Conversely, runners coming from motion control or traditional running shoes will feel right at home.
    • The Ghost 15 is specifically designed as a road running shoe. While it handles groomed gravel paths and packed dirt well, it lacks the outsole lugs and lateral stability for technical trail running. Brooks makes the Cascadia for trail work. Also worth noting: these photos on Amazon.ca show the men's version specifically — women's Ghost 15 has a different fit profile and is listed separately.

    Not for speed work

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Brooks Ghost 15 is the best daily training shoe for neutral runners who want reliable cushioning, a smooth heel-to-toe transition, and genuine durability at a reasonable price. It's not the shoe for speed work or technical trails, but for the easy and moderate miles that make up 80% of most runners' training, it's as good as it gets at this price. Wide width availability makes it accessible to runners that most shoes leave behind.
