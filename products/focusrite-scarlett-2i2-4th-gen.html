    ✅ What Works

    • 69 dB gain is the highest in this price tier — more gain headroom matters for low-output microphones like dynamic mics, ribbon mics, and instruments with low output. The 2i2 4th Gen handles these sources without noise floor issues that plague lower-gain competitors.
    • Rock-solid driver stability across Mac and Windows — the Scarlett series has the best cross-platform driver track record of any audio interface. Focusrite maintains driver support for years after product launch, including through OS updates, and the class-compliant USB mode works without any driver at all.
    • Air mode adds a subtle high-frequency transformer-style enhancement — the Air toggle adds the character of a vintage transformer-coupled preamp to the signal path. For vocals and acoustic instruments, it adds presence and air that would otherwise require post-processing.
    • Direct monitoring at < 2.7ms latency eliminates the performer-monitoring problem — at near-zero latency, singers and guitarists can monitor their live performance through headphones while recording without the distraction of hearing their voice delayed.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Scarlett Solo (1 input) is $40 cheaper if you only need one input — the 2i2's second combo input is only necessary if recording two simultaneous sources (e.g., guitar + vocal, stereo instrument). Single-source recordists should consider the Solo.
    • Onboard mixer shows only two channels — the 2i2 has 2 inputs and 2 outputs, which is the designed capacity. Recording more than 2 simultaneous channels requires the 4i4 or larger Scarlett models.
    • Ableton Live Lite included but limited — the bundled Ableton version is real Ableton software (not a trial) but limited to 8 tracks and fewer features than the full Suite. It's fully functional for learning and producing, but committed Ableton users will want the full version ($599+).
    • USB-C cable included — the 4th Gen Scarlett 2i2 uses USB-C and includes a cable. It's bus-powered (no external power adapter), and the USB-C connector is more reliable than the USB micro found on earlier generations.
