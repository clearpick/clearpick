    ✅ What Works

    • IP68 waterproofing is the highest rating in its class — fully submersible for 30 minutes at 1.5m depth; handles pool splashes, beach sand, and rain without concern.
    • 20W output produces sound that fills a medium room — the Flip 7's dual drivers and passive radiator deliver bass that punches well above its size and price.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Sound is loud but not room-filling for large spaces — the Flip 7 is perfect for outdoor gatherings of 8–10 people; for larger events or rooms, consider the Bose SoundLink Max.
    • PartyBoost allows daisy-chaining multiple JBL speakers — useful if you own two JBL speakers for wider coverage; limited to other PartyBoost-compatible JBL models.

    Common complaints

    Bass can be boomy at high volumes

    At 80%+ volume, the passive radiator overdoes the bass in some tracks. Reducing bass via the JBL app improves the balance.

    Source: Reddit (r/audiophile)

    Lacks USB-A passthrough for phone charging

    The Flip 7 removed the USB-A output from previous Flip versions — you can no longer charge a phone from the speaker's battery.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Carrying loop is basic

    The rubber carrying loop is functional but less sturdy than competitors like the UE Wonderboom's 360° loop. Not a dealbreaker but noted.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
    ClearPick Verdict
