    ✅ What Works

    • 4K/60fps 10-bit at under $1,000 with IBIS is a category-defining specification — this combination of resolution, frame rate, bit depth, and stabilization was previously only available on cameras costing $1,500+. Content creators get professional-grade video capability at a beginner-accessible price.
    • AI subject recognition tracks faces and eyes with class-leading reliability for content creation — when vlogging while walking or presenting to camera, the ZV-E10 II maintains subject lock without manual AF point adjustment.
    • Product Showcase mode automatically shifts focus from the creator's face to objects brought in front of the camera — eliminating the autofocus hunting that plagues product demonstrations on standard cameras. Uniquely useful for review creators, cooking channels, and tutorial content.
    • Directional 3-capsule microphone captures forward-facing audio with natural-sounding wind noise reduction — the on-board microphone is genuinely good enough for casual content creation without an external mic.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • No viewfinder — the ZV-E10 II is designed for screen-based composition. In very bright sunlight, the screen can be difficult to see. A clip-on EVF is available as an accessory but adds cost.
    • Smaller battery than the full a6700 — the compact body uses a smaller battery pack that provides approximately 440 shots per charge, less than the a6700's rating. Carry a spare for heavy video days.
    • Sony E-mount gives access to the largest third-party AF lens ecosystem — Sigma and Tamron both make well-reviewed APS-C lenses for E-mount at prices below Sony's own glass.
    • The ZV-E10 II is the successor to the original ZV-E10, with IBIS being the major addition — if you already own the original ZV-E10, the IBIS upgrade alone may justify the switch.
