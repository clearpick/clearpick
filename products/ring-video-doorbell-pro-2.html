    ✅ What Works

    • 1536p Head-to-Toe HD+ video with 1:1 square ratio — packages on the ground and full-body views are visible where other doorbells cut them off. A practical improvement over standard 16:9 doorbell cameras.
    • Radar-based 3D motion detection significantly reduces false alerts compared to older PIR-based models — reviewers report near-zero false triggers for cars and pedestrians after tuning motion zones.
    • Color night vision using ambient porch or street light delivers noticeably better night footage than infrared-only competitors, though performance degrades in complete darkness.
    • Tight Alexa ecosystem integration — Echo speakers announce visitors aloud, Echo Show displays live feed automatically.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Recording requires a Ring Protect subscription — without it, you get live view only. No saved clips, no person or package alerts. In Canada: Ring Solo $5.99/mo or $59.99/yr (one device); Ring Multi $14.99/mo or $149.99/yr (all devices). There is no local storage option.
    • Wired installation only — requires a 16–24V transformer with at least 40 VA. Many older Canadian homes have underpowered 16V/10VA transformers that cause connectivity and feature failures. Budget for potential transformer replacement.
    • People-only detection is unreliable — reviewers found it unable to consistently distinguish between humans and pets, triggering false alerts despite the advanced radar setup.
    • Night vision in complete darkness is limited to approximately 15 feet of usable range — fine detail beyond that goes soft. The color vision advantage applies only when ambient light is present.
