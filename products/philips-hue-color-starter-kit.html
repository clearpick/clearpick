    ✅ What Works

    • The color range is genuinely exceptional — 16 million colors spanning warm candlelight (2000K) to crisp daylight (6500K) plus full RGB saturation. The on-screen color picker translates faithfully to what you see in the room, with no visible banding or color shift at different brightness levels.
    • Because Hue operates over Zigbee through the Bridge rather than congested Wi-Fi, automations and voice commands execute near-instantly even in dense wireless environments. The Bridge also allows local control, so lights respond when your internet is down — something many Wi-Fi bulb competitors can’t match.
    • The Hue app is the most mature in the smart bulb category. Scenes, schedules, geofencing, and the Entertainment API for sync with TV and games all work reliably. Third-party integrations with HomeKit, SmartThings, and major voice assistants are deep and stable.
    • The starter kit includes everything needed to get started — four color bulbs and the Bridge — with no additional hub purchases or subscriptions required. Adding more bulbs later is straightforward, and Hue’s product line extends to outdoor lights, light bars, and gradient strips on the same ecosystem.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The Bridge is required for most features and represents a single point of failure — if it goes offline, all automations and remote access stop working. Most users set it and forget it, but it is an extra device to manage and place near your router.
    • At $199 CAD for four bulbs and the Bridge, the per-bulb cost is high compared to Wi-Fi competitors. Expanding to a full house gets expensive quickly — individual Hue color bulbs run $35–60 CAD each at regular prices.
    • Hue’s Entertainment sync (Hue Sync app for gaming and video) requires a separate Hue Sync Box or PC app — it’s not built into the Bridge. The setup is impressive when it works, but adds another device and subscription consideration for that use case.
    • The 800-lumen output per bulb is on the lower end for a room-primary light. In large spaces or ceiling fixtures with multiple sockets, the color vibrancy is great but raw brightness may not replace a standard 1100-lumen A19 adequately.
