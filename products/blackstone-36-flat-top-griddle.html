    ✅ What Works

    • The 720 square inch cold-rolled steel cooking surface is the star of this griddle. Once properly seasoned, it develops a non-stick patina that outperforms most dedicated non-stick pans, and the flat surface lets you cook everything from smash burgers and bacon to stir fry and eggs simultaneously across four independent heat zones.
    • Four independently controlled H-shaped burners give you tremendous flexibility. You can run a screaming-hot sear zone on one side while keeping pancakes warm on the other — a level of temperature zoning that traditional grill grates simply cannot match.
    • The 60,000 BTU output heats up fast and recovers quickly after you load cold food. Most users report the surface reaching cooking temperature in under 10 minutes, and the heat distribution across the full surface is remarkably even compared to round burner designs.
    • Folding legs and the relatively compact footprint make this practical to move and store even in a smaller backyard. Setup is straightforward with no tools required beyond the initial assembly, and the push-and-turn electronic ignition is reliable and easy to use.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Cold-rolled steel requires seasoning before first use and ongoing maintenance to prevent rust — especially in Canadian climates with humidity swings. Budget for a dedicated Blackstone griddle cover, and dry the surface and apply a thin oil coat after every cook. This is a commitment compared to a standard grill.
    • The rear grease management channel drains to a small cup on the back-right corner. It works, but the cup is small and can overflow during heavy bacon or fatty meat sessions. Many owners upgrade to a drip bucket attachment or simply check it frequently during long cooks.
    • There is no lid on this model, which means you cannot trap heat for melting cheese or cooking thicker items through indirect heat. If you want a lid, you'll need to buy an aftermarket option or step up to the Blackstone model that includes one.
    • Propane tank is not included, and at 60,000 BTU you will burn through a standard 20 lb tank faster than a typical gas grill. Canadian winters also mean propane pressure can drop in very cold weather, affecting performance. Keep a spare tank on hand for long cook sessions.

    Common complaints

    Rust appears quickly without proper care

    Multiple buyers report surface rust forming within weeks if the griddle is left uncovered or not oiled after cooking. Cold-rolled steel demands consistent maintenance that some buyers don't expect from an outdoor appliance.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Grease cup overflows easily

    The rear grease management cup is small relative to the cooking surface area. During high-fat cooks like bacon for a crowd, it can overflow and create a mess or a flare-up hazard underneath.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Wind affects cooking temperature significantly

    Without a lid or windscreen, breezy days noticeably reduce surface temperature and create uneven hot and cold zones. Several Reddit users recommend buying a windscreen accessory if you cook in an exposed area.

    Source: Reddit

    Legs can be wobbly on uneven ground

    The folding leg design, while convenient for storage, does not level on anything other than a flat hard surface. On a deck with minor slope or patio pavers, the griddle can rock noticeably.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
