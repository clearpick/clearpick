    ✅ What Works

    • Milwaukee's POWERSTATE brushless motor delivers 1,200 in-lbs of torque — a figure that leads the 18V hammer drill category. This peak torque translates to driving 3/8-inch structural lag bolts into dense LVL and glulam beams without the motor bogging, which is the real-world test that separates high-torque drills from spec-sheet drills that bog under load.
    • Three-speed gearbox (0-550/0-2,000/0-3,100 RPM) gives fine precision control in speed 1 for masonry anchors and delicate driving, middle gear (2,000 RPM) for general drilling and driving, and top gear (3,100 RPM) for speed-critical tasks like drilling large-diameter holes in softwood. Most drills offer two speeds; the third is a meaningful addition for trades doing varied drilling tasks throughout the day.
    • REDLINK PLUS electronics prevent motor burnout under overload conditions — if the drill encounters resistance that would damage the motor (stalls in dense material, excessive sustained load), the electronics cut power before damage occurs. This protection extends tool service life substantially beyond tools without overload electronics, which burn out under the kinds of loads contractors impose daily.
    • 58,500 BPM in hammer mode is the highest percussion rate in this drill class, enabling faster and cleaner anchor drilling in brick, block, and light concrete. The combination of high BPM and high torque means most light masonry fastening — setting anchors in brick for ledger boards, installing masonry screws in block — doesn't require a separate SDS rotary hammer.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The 2804-20 is tool-only — no battery or charger. Milwaukee M18 batteries are premium-quality but premium-priced: a 5.0Ah REDLITHIUM is $120–$150 CAD and the M18 rapid charger adds $70–$90 CAD. All-in, a first-time Milwaukee buyer pays $470–$520 CAD for drill + battery + charger. For existing M18 users, the tool-only price is very competitive.
    • This is a hammer drill, not a rotary hammer. The cam-clutch percussion in a hammer drill is effective in brick, block, and mortar but generates only a fraction of the impact energy of an SDS rotary hammer. For drilling into poured-in-place structural concrete (parking structures, foundations, slabs), an SDS Plus or SDS Max rotary hammer is the appropriate tool.
    • At 4.9 lbs (tool only, before battery), the 2804-20 is on the heavier end for 18V drills. A 5.0Ah battery adds 1.5 lbs, putting the full assembly at 6.4 lbs. For overhead drilling over extended periods, a lighter 2.0Ah or 3.0Ah battery and planned task rotation reduces fatigue.
    • The REDLINK PLUS system occasionally interprets legitimate high-load tasks (driving large lag bolts into green lumber, drilling large-diameter holes in hardwood) as overload events and momentarily cuts power. This is protective behaviour, not a malfunction — completing the cut in smaller increments resolves it, and the system re-enables immediately.
