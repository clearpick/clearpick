    ✅ What Works

    • Battery and charger are included in the box — a key advantage over EGO and Ryobi at this price tier, where tool-only listings are common. What you see is what you pay.
    • At 17" and ~50 lb, this is one of the lighter cordless mowers available. Maneuvering around garden beds, trees, and tight corners is noticeably easier than heavier 21" alternatives.
    • The 40V brushless motor handles standard residential grass at a pace matching the 35–45 min runtime. For a typical small urban Canadian lot (under 2,000 sq ft), one charge is usually enough.
    • Quiet enough to use early morning or in the evening without angering neighbours — noticeably quieter than gas and comparable to most battery alternatives.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The 17" cutting width means significantly more passes than a 21" deck — on a 3,000+ sq ft lot, the extra time adds up and may exhaust the battery before finishing.
    • Push-only with no self-propel — on anything but flat ground, you're providing all the motive force. Not ideal for sloped Canadian properties.
    • The 35–45 min runtime is honest for most users, but heavy or wet grass will shorten it. Don't plan on a single charge covering more than a small lot.
    • The Greenworks 40V ecosystem is smaller than DEWALT 20V or EGO 56V — fewer compatible tools if you want to expand into blowers, trimmers, and snow blowers later.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Greenworks 40V 17" is the best value entry point into cordless mowing — battery and charger included at $399 CAD beats most competitors at this price. The 17" deck and push-only drive are real limitations that will frustrate anyone with a medium or larger lot, and the 40V ecosystem has fewer expansion options than EGO or DEWALT. For small, flat urban lots under 2,000 sq ft, it's a practical, affordable choice. For anything larger or sloped, the EGO LM2156SP is worth the premium.
