    ✅ What Works

    • The Rainmaker shower head distributes water through 26 perforations across the full surface of the coffee bed, ensuring even saturation without dry channels — the most common cause of flat, under-extracted pour-over coffee.
    • The flat-bottom filter basket (Melitta-style) produces more even extraction than a conical V60 for beginners because it slows the flow rate and allows more contact time, making it more forgiving of inconsistent pouring technique.
    • The water tank design allows you to pour all the hot water in at once rather than manually circling in stages — it approximates the pour-over experience with less technique required.
    • The thermal carafe lid keeps coffee hot for 30+ minutes after brewing without a warming plate, eliminating the burnt-coffee smell of drip machines left on keep-warm.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Requires a separate kettle to heat water — the OXO Brew itself cannot heat water, so it adds a step vs. a standard drip machine.
    • Uses Melitta No. 4 flat-bottom filters — not compatible with Chemex or V60 filters, and you'll need to keep Melitta filters on hand (available at most Canadian grocery stores).
    • The borosilicate glass carafe is high quality but not indestructible — a few owners have reported breakage from drops or thermal shock, which is common with glass vessels.
    • Pour-over coffee quality depends on the grind, water temperature, and coffee freshness more than any manual equipment — the OXO Brew is an excellent vessel, but great results still require quality inputs.
