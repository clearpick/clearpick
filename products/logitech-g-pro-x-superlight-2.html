    ✅ What Works

    • 60 grams is the gold standard for lightweight wireless mice, and the SUPERLIGHT 2 achieves it without sacrificing structural rigidity. The shell does not flex under grip pressure, the click buttons do not rattle, and the material quality matches premium peripherals that weigh 30-40 grams more. Logitech's engineering achievement here is making a 60g mouse that feels substantial rather than cheap.
    • The HERO 2 sensor with zero smoothing, filtering, or acceleration is the most accurate gaming sensor currently shipping in a consumer product. At any DPI setting from 100 to 32,000, the cursor tracks your physical hand movement with one-to-one precision — no prediction, no interpolation, no artificial smoothing that masks micro-tremors or introduces inconsistency. Professional players choose this sensor specifically because it gives them the truest representation of their own mechanics.
    • LIGHTFORCE hybrid optical-mechanical switches represent a meaningful advance in click technology. Standard mechanical switches have a debounce delay — a brief pause after the initial contact to prevent double-registering the vibration of the click mechanism. LIGHTFORCE uses an optical confirmation layer to detect intentional clicks faster, eliminating ~2ms of latency that mechanical switches inherently carry. In practice this means more responsive registration in games where click timing matters.
    • 95-hour battery life with LIGHTSPEED wireless is exceptional — the original SUPERLIGHT's 70 hours was already class-leading, and the SUPERLIGHT 2 extends it to nearly four days of 8-hour daily gaming on a single charge. Combined with USB-C charging (and passtrough play-while-charging support), this mouse effectively never needs to be powered down for battery reasons.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The SUPERLIGHT 2 has a subtle right-hand ergonomic bias in its shape despite being marketed as symmetrical. Left-handed users can use it comfortably, but the button layout is optimized for right-hand orientation. True ambidextrous options like the Razer DeathAdder V3 Hyperspeed or Logitech G305 are better choices for left-handed gamers.
    • No onboard RGB lighting — the SUPERLIGHT 2 is a purely performance-focused mouse with no light effects. If RGB aesthetics are important to your setup, this is explicitly not the right choice — Logitech's G502 X Plus or G403 provide RGB alongside competitive performance. The SUPERLIGHT 2 is designed around weight and performance, not visual appeal.
    • LIGHTSPEED wireless requires the USB dongle to maintain connection. The dongle is compact and can plug into a USB hub or the Logitech G POWERPLAY charging mat if your desktop has limited front USB access. Signal stability is excellent and does not degrade near WiFi routers or Bluetooth devices, but the dongle must remain within ~10 meters of the mouse.
    • The mouse is not water-resistant — it does not have an IP rating. Accidental spills should be avoided and the mouse should not be used in high-humidity environments. The PTFE feet are replaceable if worn, and Logitech sells replacement sets for long-term ownership.

    Common complaints

    No RGB — purely utilitarian

    The SUPERLIGHT 2's lack of RGB is a deliberate design choice for weight savings, but it generates consistent complaints from buyers who expected the aesthetic options available on other premium gaming mice. The mouse is a tool, not a desk decoration.

    Source: Reddit

    Side buttons could be more pronounced

    Some users find the side thumb buttons have less tactile feedback than competitors. The buttons are functional and reliable, but the click feel is shallower than Razer's side buttons, which some gamers notice coming from other mice.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Price premium versus competitors

    At $189 CAD, the SUPERLIGHT 2 is priced at the top of the wireless gaming mouse market. Buyers comparing it to the Razer DeathAdder V3 HyperSpeed ($119) or Logitech G305 ($79) struggle to justify the premium for performance improvements that require very precise mechanical skill to perceive.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    No dongle storage in mouse

    Unlike some wireless mice that store the USB dongle inside the battery compartment, the SUPERLIGHT 2 has no dongle storage. The dongle is small enough to lose easily, and several buyers have replaced it after misplacing it during travel.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
