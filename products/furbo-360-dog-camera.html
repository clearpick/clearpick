    ✅ What Works

    • The 360° pan rotation is the killer feature over standard fixed pet cameras — you can track your dog anywhere in the room remotely, follow them from their bed to the window, and check every corner without physically repositioning the camera. The motor is quiet enough that most dogs don't react to the movement.
    • Treat tossing works reliably for most owners, and the range is surprisingly good — treats land 1–2 feet from the device consistently. Tossing a treat from a work meeting while watching your dog on the app is a genuinely fun way to interact with your pet remotely, and many dogs learn quickly that a certain notification sound means a treat is coming.
    • Barking alerts are faster than competing cameras — most owners report a 10–20 second delay between barking starting and the push notification arriving. For separation anxiety management or monitoring a dog prone to nuisance barking while you're away, this near-real-time alert is more useful than cameras that notify 60+ seconds later.
    • 1080p video quality holds up well in daylight and adequate in medium-lit rooms. Night vision switches automatically and provides enough resolution to see what your dog is doing clearly, though colour depth is lost in full darkness as expected.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Furbo's cloud features — extended video history, smart alerts, and Doggy Diary summaries — require a subscription. The basic live view is free, but most of the differentiated features cost $8–12/month CAD. Without a subscription the Furbo is a functional but expensive basic camera.
    • Treat capacity is only around 100 small treats, and the opening only accepts treats up to a specific small size. Many standard dog treats are too large — owners need to buy specific small training treats, and the hopper needs refilling every few days for frequent users.
    • The 360° motor occasionally produces a soft whirring noise when panning that sensitive dogs notice. Dogs that are already anxious may find the moving camera unsettling rather than comforting.
    • Wi-Fi reliability matters significantly for this device — a weak or unstable Wi-Fi signal in the room where you place it leads to buffering, failed treat tosses, and missed alerts. Placement near a router or a Wi-Fi extender is recommended.
