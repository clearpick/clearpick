    ✅ What Works

    • 249g weight is the critical number in Canada — sub-250g drones qualify for basic operations rules under Transport Canada, meaning recreational flyers can fly in many more locations without advanced certification. This practical advantage is the Mini 3 Pro's most important feature.
    • 4K/60fps 10-bit with a 1/1.3-inch sensor at 249g is technically impressive — the image quality rivals much heavier drones. 10-bit log footage gives videographers real colour grading flexibility.
    • Vertical shooting orientation is native — the camera physically rotates 90° for portrait-mode video, which produces true vertical footage for Instagram Reels and TikTok rather than a cropped horizontal signal.
    • Strong resale ecosystem — the Mini 3 Pro has been on the market long enough to have excellent third-party accessory support and a robust used market if you want to upgrade later.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • DJI O3 transmission vs O4 in the Mini 4 Pro — shorter range and slightly less interference resistance. In urban environments with heavy WiFi congestion, O3 can occasionally show video feed drops where O4 would maintain connection.
    • Tri-directional obstacle avoidance only covers forward, backward, and downward — the sides are unprotected. Flying in tight spaces like forests requires more pilot attention than omnidirectional systems.
    • Mini 4 Pro is the current model at a similar price point — with omnidirectional obstacle avoidance, better transmission, and slightly longer flight time. The Mini 3 Pro is still available new at some retailers as a budget option.
    • 34-minute flight time is good but noticeably shorter than the Air 3's 46 minutes — plan accordingly for longer shoots.
