    ✅ What Works

    • Oral-B's oscillating-rotating-pulsating motion removes significantly more plaque than manual brushing — this is one of the most well-replicated findings in dental research. The Pro 1000 delivers the same core motion technology as the $229 iO Series 9, just without the AI, app, or multiple modes. For plaque removal, which is the actual goal of toothbrushing, you're getting professional-level results at budget pricing.
    • The visible pressure sensor is the most important feature on this toothbrush. When you brush too hard, the indicator ring turns red and the pulsation stops — immediately halting the motion that could damage gums and enamel. This one feature prevents the most common long-term harm from electric toothbrush use.
    • The 2-minute timer with 30-second quadrant alerts enforces the proper brushing protocol that most people skip. The brush vibrates briefly at 30-second intervals signaling you to move to the next quadrant, then buzzes again at 2 minutes for stop. After a few weeks, you develop muscle memory for the timing without needing the alerts.
    • At $79 CAD with genuine clinical plaque-removal performance, a pressure sensor, a built-in timer, and universal compatibility with the entire Oral-B brush head ecosystem, the Pro 1000 has essentially no real competition in its price tier. It's the standard recommendation for anyone starting out with electric toothbrushes.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Single brushing mode (Daily Clean) means no switching between Sensitive, Whitening, or Gum Care modes. For most people this is irrelevant — Daily Clean is the mode you'd use 90% of the time anyway. But if you have specific needs like post-surgery sensitivity or whitening goals, the iO Series 9's multi-mode flexibility matters.
    • Battery life of 5 days is the weakest spec on this brush. If you travel and forget to pack your charger, you could run out mid-trip. The solution is to travel with a charged backup head (which works with any Oral-B charger) or to keep a separate travel charger. The Oral-B charger is standard and available at pharmacies.
    • The charger is a contact-charging stand, not the magnetic inductive charging of the iO Series. This means a two-pin base that the handle sits on — secure but slightly more fiddly than the iO's charging. Not a dealbreaker but worth knowing.
    • Replacement CrossAction brush heads run $6–10 CAD each and are widely available at drugstores and grocery stores across Canada. The broad availability and lower head cost than iO brush heads is a meaningful practical advantage of the Pro 1000 platform.
