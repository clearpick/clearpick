    ✅ What Works

    • The OPX optical-mechanical switches are the K100's defining feature. Using a light beam to detect keystrokes instead of physical metal contact, they achieve 1.0mm actuation depth — the fastest available in a Corsair keyboard — while eliminating the debounce delay inherent to traditional mechanical switches. In fast-paced competitive games, the response advantage is real and measurable.
    • The iCUE Control Wheel on the upper left is genuinely useful rather than a gimmick. It can be assigned to system volume, individual application volume, mouse DPI, lighting brightness, media scrubbing, or even custom macros — and the assignment is per-profile. Content creators who switch between gaming and production work particularly appreciate having a tactile dial to adjust parameters without leaving their keyboard.
    • Build quality is flagship-tier. The full aluminum top plate with brushed finish gives the K100 a premium feel that is noticeably more solid than keyboards with plastic frames. At $279 CAD it is positioned as a luxury peripheral, and the construction matches the price expectation — it feels like something built to last a decade.
    • Corsair iCUE software gives granular RGB programming and per-key macro assignment that rivals any keyboard on the market. The 8MB onboard memory stores up to three profiles independently of the software, meaning your custom lighting and macros travel with the keyboard. The 4,000Hz polling mode puts input update rate at four times the standard 1,000Hz if your PC can take advantage of it.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The K100 is a full-size keyboard with a numpad and a media control row, which means it is large and takes up significant desk space. If you run a tenkeyless setup to bring your mouse closer to center, the SteelSeries Apex Pro TKL or Logitech G915 TKL are the better choices. The K100 does not offer a TKL variant with optical switches.
    • Corsair iCUE software is comprehensive but resource-intensive. Some users with lower-spec systems have reported that iCUE runs noticeably in the background, particularly during startup. The onboard memory profiles work without the software, but RGB customization and advanced macro management require iCUE running.
    • OPX switches have a linear feel with no tactile bump, which is ideal for gaming but not necessarily preferred for heavy typists. If you spend significant time typing documents or code, the lack of tactile feedback may be less satisfying than a tactile or clicky mechanical switch like Cherry MX Brown or Kailh Box White.
    • The K100 is wired-only — there is no wireless variant. The USB passthrough requires a second USB port on your PC. For cable-conscious setups, the desk presence and wire management requirements should be considered before purchase.

    Common complaints

    iCUE software is resource-heavy

    Multiple users reported that Corsair iCUE significantly increases CPU usage and system startup time. While the keyboard works perfectly without the software via onboard memory, accessing the full feature set requires keeping iCUE running, which some users find unacceptable on gaming PCs.

    Source: Reddit

    Full-size only — no TKL option

    The K100's full-size layout with macro keys and control wheel is a dealbreaker for mouse-space-conscious gamers who want a tenkeyless footprint. Corsair does not offer a TKL version of the K100, which limits its appeal for competitive FPS setups.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Premium price vs alternatives

    At $279 CAD, several reviewers noted that the Logitech G915 TKL Wireless at $229 and SteelSeries Apex Pro TKL at $279 offer compelling alternatives, especially given the G915's wireless capability and the Apex Pro's adjustable actuation — features the K100 does not match.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Wrist rest sold separately

    For a keyboard at this price point, several buyers expected a wrist rest to be included. Corsair sells a compatible wrist rest separately for an additional $30+, which felt like a nickel-and-dime move on an already premium product.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
    ClearPick Verdict

    The Corsair K100 RGB is the best full-size optical-mechanical gaming keyboard currently available, combining the fastest switches in Corsair's lineup with flagship build quality, a genuinely useful iCUE Control Wheel, and comprehensive software support. The full-size-only format and iCUE software overhead are real considerations, but for gamers and content creators who want the absolute top of the full-size keyboard tier, the K100 delivers without compromise.
