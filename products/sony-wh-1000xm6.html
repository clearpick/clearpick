    ✅ What Works

    • Best-in-class noise cancellation — the new QN3 processor with Adaptive NC Optimizer adjusts in real-time to ambient sound and fit, making it noticeably more effective than the XM5 in unpredictable environments.
    • Sony fixed the XM5's fragile hinge: the XM6 folds flat like the XM4 with visible metal reinforcement, making them genuinely travel-safe in a way the XM5 was not.
    • 37+ hours of ANC battery life — among the longest of any flagship noise-cancelling headphone — and you can now use them while charging via USB-C, which wasn't possible on the XM5.
    • Sound quality is meaningfully improved: Sony re-engineered the 30mm drivers and smoothed out the XM5's harsh treble peaks, resulting in a warmer, more listenable sound signature that works well without EQ.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The ear pads feel deflated out of the box compared to the XM5, and the ANC mic protrudes slightly inside the cup, which causes rubbing on larger ears during extended sessions — a real comfort regression from the XM5 for some users.
    • At ~$600 CAD, the XM6 is a premium purchase. If you already own XM5s in good condition, the upgrade is worthwhile mainly for the improved folding hinge and ANC — not a must-buy if your XM5s are intact.
    • Multi-point Bluetooth connects to two devices simultaneously, but audio handoff between devices still requires a manual tap in the Sony app — not as seamless as some competitors.

    ClearPick Verdict

    Owners who switched from cracked or damaged XM5s say the XM6 is a clear upgrade — the metal-reinforced hinge is the first thing they mention, followed by the ANC improvement. Sound reviews land the same way: the harsh treble that XM5 owners learned to EQ around is noticeably smoother here. If your XM5s are intact and the hinge hasn't failed, this is a lateral move. If you're buying new in the category, or your XM5 hinge cracked, this is the one.
