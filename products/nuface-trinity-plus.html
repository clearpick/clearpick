    ✅ What Works

    • Microcurrent technology delivers low-level electrical current that mirrors the body's own bioelectric field. This stimulates the ATP production in facial muscles that drives toning, tightening, and re-educating sagging facial muscles. It's FDA-cleared — not just FDA-registered, which is a meaningful distinction in the at-home beauty device category where many products claim regulatory backing they don't have.
    • The ELE attachment's smaller applicator spheres are specifically designed for the delicate eye and lip area where the main device's sphere size is too large for precise treatment. Users who skip the ELE attachment miss out on targeted lifting around the eyes and definition around the lip border, which are often the first areas people notice laxity.
    • The Wrinkle Reducer attachment adds red light therapy to the microcurrent — red light at 660nm wavelength has solid peer-reviewed evidence for stimulating collagen production and reducing fine line depth. Having both modalities in one device is genuine value; standalone red light devices run $100–300 CAD separately.
    • The 60-day visible results timeline holds up in independent testing. Users who commit to the 5-minute daily protocol for two months consistently report visible improvements in jaw definition, brow lift, and cheek lift that hold up in before-and-after photography.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The NuFACE Gel Primer is required for conductivity — without it the microcurrent cannot properly penetrate the skin. The gel costs $30–40 CAD per bottle and lasts about 30–60 days of daily use, making it an ongoing consumable that adds $180–480 CAD annually. Factor this into the total cost calculation before purchasing.
    • Results are maintenance-dependent, not permanent. After the initial 60-day intensive phase, you need 2–3 treatments per week to maintain the results. If you stop using the device, the toning effects reverse over several weeks. This is similar to gym results — you have to keep doing it.
    • At $489 CAD for the Trinity+ bundle, this is among the most expensive at-home beauty devices in this guide. Clinical in-office microcurrent treatments run $150–300 per session; the device pays for itself after 3–4 sessions if you'd otherwise seek professional treatments.
    • Microcurrent is contraindicated for certain conditions including pacemakers, pregnancy, epilepsy, metal facial implants, and active acne/rosacea flares. Check the full contraindication list before purchasing — NuFACE documents these thoroughly in the product manual.
