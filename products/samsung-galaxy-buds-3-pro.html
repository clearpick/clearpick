    ✅ What Works

    • IP57 water resistance is the strongest in the premium TWS tier — the buds can survive brief submersion, not just sweat. A genuine advantage for outdoor workouts and wet conditions.
    • Dual-driver design (planar tweeter + dynamic woofer) delivers impressive treble extension and wide soundstage. Objective scores put sound quality on par with the AirPods Pro 2 and Sony XM5.
    • Galaxy AI features are legitimately useful for Samsung phone owners — real-time live interpreter, super-wideband calls, 360 Audio on Samsung TVs, and seamless auto-switching between Galaxy phone and Galaxy PC.
    • 9-band EQ in the companion app with Adaptive Noise Control, Find My Buds, and full Galaxy AI feature access for compatible Samsung devices.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • ANC is measurably weaker than both the Sony XM5 and AirPods Pro 2 — SoundGuys measured only 76% noise reduction, and reviewers note you can still hear conversations clearly with ANC on. This is a regression from the Buds2 Pro, not an improvement.
    • Real-world battery falls well short of spec — SoundGuys tested 4h 31min with ANC on vs. Samsung's claimed 6 hours. Meaningful for full-day users.
    • Advanced features are Samsung-only — SSC Hi-Fi codec, Galaxy AI interpreter, 360 Audio, and auto-switching require a recent Samsung flagship. iPhone and non-Samsung Android users pay $329 for a stripped-down experience.
    • Samsung halted shipments shortly after launch due to production quality control issues. Shipments have since resumed but the launch stumble is worth noting.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Galaxy Buds 3 Pro are a solid pair of earbuds built almost entirely for Samsung Galaxy phone owners. The dual-driver sound quality is genuinely impressive, IP57 rating beats everything else in the TWS tier, and Galaxy AI features are useful if you travel internationally. The problem: ANC is a step backward from the Buds2 Pro, real-world battery falls well short of Samsung's claim, and the best features are locked behind Samsung hardware. On iPhone or non-Samsung Android, you're paying $329 for about half a product.
