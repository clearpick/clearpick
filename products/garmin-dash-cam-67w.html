    ✅ What Works

    • The 180-degree horizontal field of view captures the full road across three lanes in each direction—a significant advantage over 140-degree cameras that lose coverage at lane edges; particularly valuable for documenting lane-change incidents and wide-angle parking lot footage
    • Built-in GPS records speed and exact location on every frame of footage, providing objective evidence for insurance claims and legal disputes without requiring manual timestamp reconciliation
    • Voice commands work reliably in a noisy car cabin—'OK Garmin, save video' saves the current clip without taking your hands off the wheel, which is the right workflow at the moment of an incident when you're already stressed
    • Garmin's driver assistance alerts (lane departure warning, forward collision alert, go alert) are accurate enough to be useful rather than annoyingly over-sensitive, which is a real differentiator from cheaper Chinese dash cam ADAS systems

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • 1440p is excellent but not 4K; if capturing clear license plates at highway distances in low light is the priority, consider the Thinkware U1000 or VIOFO A139 Pro's 4K sensor for sharper long-range text
    • The 180-degree lens creates mild barrel distortion at the extreme edges of the frame—footage looks natural in the center two-thirds but edges curve visibly; this rarely affects incident documentation but is noticeable in side-by-side comparisons
    • No rear camera in this model—the 67W is front-only; pair with a separate rear camera or consider the BlackVue DR970X-2CH if dual-channel coverage is needed

    Common complaints

    No included memory card

    The 67W is sold without a microSD card despite Garmin recommending a specific card tier (U3 rated) for continuous recording. A quality 64–128GB card adds $20–$40 CAD to the total purchase cost.

    Source: Amazon.ca reviewer

    App connectivity can be finicky

    The Garmin Drive app Bluetooth connection drops occasionally on some Android phones, requiring a re-pair. iOS connectivity is consistently reported as more stable than Android in user reviews.

    Source: Reddit r/Dashcam

    Front-only recording

    At $200, buyers expect the option for rear camera coverage. The 67W requires a companion rear camera (sold separately) for full coverage—total cost with rear camera approaches $350–$400.

    Source: Reddit r/Dashcam
    ClearPick Verdict

    The Garmin Dash Cam 67W is the best single-channel dash cam for drivers who want the widest possible coverage with the reliability and polish of a premium brand. The 180-degree field of view, GPS integration, and voice commands are a genuinely better implementation than competing Chinese brands at the same price. The 4K gap matters for long-range license plate capture; the VIOFO A139 Pro matches or beats the 67W for front-only 4K recording at a similar price. Choose the 67W if the Garmin ecosystem, driver alerts, and brand support matter to you.
