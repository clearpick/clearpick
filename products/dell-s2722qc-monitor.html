    ✅ What Works

    • At around $449 CAD, the S2722QC brings 4K IPS quality and USB-C with 65W Power Delivery to a price point that previously required accepting 1440p or compromising on connectivity. For most office setups, this is the right amount of monitor at the right price.
    • The USB-C port with 65W Power Delivery handles both display and laptop charging through a single cable — clean desk setups with a single cable connecting laptop to monitor work perfectly on MacBook Air, Dell XPS 13, and most USB-C ultrabooks.
    • The 99% sRGB coverage and IPS panel produce accurate, consistent colors across the screen with excellent viewing angles. Document work, photo review, and video calls all look sharp and true, making this a genuine step up from budget FHD monitors.
    • The stand adjustment covers height, tilt, and swivel but not pivot to portrait. For users who work in landscape orientation exclusively, the ergonomic range is sufficient. The stand is study and doesn't creep once set.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The stand does not pivot to portrait orientation, which matters for developers and readers who value portrait mode for long documents. The VESA mount (100×100) accepts aftermarket arms if portrait is important.
    • HDR400 certification means the peak brightness for HDR content is 400 nits, which produces a modest HDR effect — better than no HDR but not comparable to true HDR monitors with local dimming above 600 nits.
    • The 60Hz refresh rate is standard for a productivity monitor at this price. If you want 4K and high refresh rate, you'll need to spend significantly more for 4K@144Hz panels.
    • Some early units had a uniformity issue with slight brightness variation in corners. This is less consistent than Dell's higher-end U-series monitors; if you receive a unit with noticeable uniformity problems, Dell's support for replacements is generally responsive.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Dell S2722QC is the most accessible 4K USB-C monitor for Canadian buyers who want single-cable laptop connectivity at a reasonable price. The IPS panel with 99% sRGB is genuinely accurate, the USB-C at 65W handles most ultrabooks, and 4K at 27 inches delivers meaningfully sharper text than 1440p. The lack of portrait pivot and 60Hz ceiling are the main limitations, but for pure office productivity this is an excellent choice at its price point.
