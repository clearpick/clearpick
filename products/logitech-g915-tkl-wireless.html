    ✅ What Works

    • Low-profile mechanical switches are the G915 TKL's defining characteristic. At 22mm total keyboard height and 2.7mm key travel (versus 4mm on standard mechanical), the G915 sits flat enough on a desk that wrist rests become optional rather than necessary. Gamers who type for extended periods and prefer a flatter hand angle consistently report less wrist strain compared to standard-height keyboards — the ergonomic benefit of low-profile is real and underappreciated.
    • LIGHTSPEED wireless at 1ms polling rate is tournament-grade, meaning the keyboard's wireless connection introduces no perceptible input latency versus a wired keyboard. Paired with Bluetooth 5.1 for dual-connect, the G915 TKL can simultaneously maintain a LIGHTSPEED connection to a gaming PC and a Bluetooth connection to a second device — switching audio or control without any re-pairing process.
    • The TKL (tenkeyless) form factor removes the numpad from the right side, shifting the keyboard left and bringing your mouse hand closer to your body's centerline. This is a meaningful ergonomic and space improvement for PC gaming: your mousing arm sits at a more natural angle and you gain significant mouse movement range without changing desk size. The G915 TKL pairs this ergonomic advantage with low-profile key height for a doubly comfortable setup.
    • Battery performance is exceptional in both modes. 40 hours with full RGB enabled is already competitive with wired keyboards that need no battery. Turn off the RGB and the G915 TKL reaches an extraordinary 1,000 hours on a single charge — making it the wireless keyboard that most closely approximates the no-battery-worry convenience of a wired keyboard. For anyone who has been frustrated by wireless keyboards dying mid-session, this figure is transformative.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Low-profile GL switches have 2.7mm total travel — about 35% less than standard MX switches at 4mm. Mechanical switch enthusiasts who are accustomed to deeper key travel may find the GL switches feel less satisfying to type on, particularly if transitioning from heavier or clicky full-travel switches. The switches are excellent for gaming and light typing but may not satisfy heavy typist preferences.
    • Five dedicated G macro keys on the left side of the keyboard are useful for MMO, strategy, and MOBA players who rely on complex multi-key macros. FPS gamers who never use macros may find the additional columns on the keyboard's left edge unnecessary, though they do not interfere with standard typing or gaming. G HUB software is required to program macro functions.
    • The aluminum top plate is excellent for durability and premium feel, but the keyboard does not include a wrist rest. For a low-profile keyboard at 22mm, a wrist rest is rarely needed — the angle is natural for most gamers — but buyers with specific wrist health requirements should note there is no bundled wrist support.
    • Dual-connect wireless means both LIGHTSPEED and Bluetooth connections can be active simultaneously for audio or notifications, but the keyboard only inputs through one connection at a time. Switching from LIGHTSPEED gaming mode to Bluetooth for a different device requires pressing the Bluetooth button on the keyboard — it is a quick action but not instantaneous.

    Common complaints

    Low-profile feel is divisive

    Experienced mechanical keyboard users who typed on Cherry MX switches for years find the GL low-profile switches less satisfying tactilely. The shorter travel and lighter actuation are well-suited to gaming but the typing feedback is qualitatively different from full-height switches, which generates polarized opinions.

    Source: Reddit

    G HUB required for full customization

    Like other Logitech G products, macro assignment, RGB profiles, and advanced wireless settings require G HUB. The software works well for most users but occasional stability issues during Windows updates frustrate a subset of buyers.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Price versus SteelSeries Apex Pro TKL

    At $229 CAD, the G915 TKL is directly priced against the SteelSeries Apex Pro TKL Wireless at $279 CAD. Several buyers note the Apex Pro's adjustable actuation OmniPoint switches are a more innovative feature than the GL switches, though the G915's 1,000-hour battery without RGB is unmatched.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Limited switch options per variant

    The G915 TKL is available in GL Linear, GL Clicky, and GL Tactile variants, but each is sold as a separate purchase. Buyers who want to try different switch types must purchase different keyboards — there is no switch testing option or modular switch design.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
