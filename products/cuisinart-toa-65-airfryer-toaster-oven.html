    ✅ What Works

    • The TOA-65 genuinely replaces both a toaster oven and an air fryer without compromising either. The convection element produces crispy air-fried results that rival dedicated basket-style air fryers, while the full oven capacity handles a 12-inch pizza or a whole roast chicken without any awkward fitting.
    • Eight cooking functions cover every common use case — from the morning toast run to roasting a chicken to dehydrating herbs. Speed Convection is particularly useful: it cuts baking times by 30–40% compared to conventional oven settings.
    • The included accessories are genuinely useful: the air fry rack elevates food for 360-degree airflow, the baking pan catches drips, and the broiling rack handles steaks and fish. Most competitors charge extra for accessories that Cuisinart includes in the box.
    • Build quality is noticeably above the price point — the stainless steel exterior, solid door hinge, and consistent temperature calibration are all traits of a unit built to last. Owners who've had it 3–5 years consistently report it's still performing to spec.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The exterior gets very hot during use — the top and sides reach temperatures that will burn you if you brush against them. If you have children in the kitchen, placement matters. The back needs at least 5 inches of clearance from the wall.
    • The digital controls require more button presses than a simple knob-and-dial setup — you select function, then set temperature, then set time. The learning curve is real for the first week, though it becomes intuitive quickly.
    • At 0.6 cubic feet, it's not a full oven replacement. A 9x13-inch baking dish doesn't fit — it tops out at 9x11. For holiday roasting or large sheet pans, you'll still need your main oven.
    • The crumb tray needs cleaning after every use if you use the toaster function heavily — crumbs build up quickly and can cause smoke if left to accumulate. Not a deal-breaker but a genuine maintenance requirement.
