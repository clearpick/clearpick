    ✅ What Works

    • Quiet for the price — 39 dBA maximum is comparable to budget tower fans from more expensive brands. On the lowest setting, the Pelonis is noticeable but not disruptive for bedroom use.
    • Sleep mode gradually reduces fan speed over a set period — as body temperature decreases during sleep, less cooling is needed. The progressive speed reduction mirrors this naturally.
    • Remote control included at $69 — many competing budget tower fans don't include a remote at this price point. The remote covers all key functions.
    • Compact footprint fits in bedroom corners without taking meaningful floor space — the slim 40-inch tower design tucks neatly against a wall.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Airflow is noticeably weaker than Honeywell and Vornado fans — the Pelonis moves less air volume, making it suitable for personal cooling and light bedroom circulation but insufficient for cooling larger spaces.
    • 70° oscillation is limited — the arc doesn't cover a full room from most bedroom placements. Positioning close to the bed maximizes effective cooling.
    • Plastic build quality is basic — the lightweight plastic chassis is functional but feels fragile compared to more expensive alternatives. Handle carefully during storage.
    • Not for large rooms or hot summers — in heat above 30°C or rooms larger than a standard bedroom, the Pelonis won't produce enough airflow to provide meaningful cooling.
