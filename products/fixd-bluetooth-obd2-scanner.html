    ✅ What Works

    • The FIXD app translates generic fault codes into plain English with an estimated severity rating—instead of 'P0420 Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold Bank 1,' you see 'Catalytic Converter Issue — Not urgent, monitor and service within 30 days'—genuinely useful for non-mechanical owners
    • Setup is the fastest in the category: plug the sensor into the OBD2 port, open the app, and you're reading codes in under 60 seconds without any Bluetooth pairing menus or manual sync procedures
    • The sensor stays plugged in permanently and actively monitors the vehicle; the app notifies you when a new code appears before a warning light illuminates in some cases, giving you a heads-up before the situation becomes obvious
    • At $89 it's significantly less than professional scanners while providing the core consumer use case—reading a check engine light before going to a mechanic so you know whether it's urgent and have context for the repair estimate

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The free app reads and clears codes but the most useful features (severity ratings, repair estimates, maintenance tracking) require FIXD Premium at approximately $8–$10 USD/month or a one-time annual fee—factor this into the total cost
    • Coverage is OBD2 gas vehicles only; diesel vehicles, hybrids with complex systems, and EVs are not reliably supported, and the sensor won't work with pre-1996 vehicles without OBD2 ports
    • Advanced diagnostics (ABS, SRS, transmission codes, live data graphs, service resets) require the Autel MK808 or similar professional-grade scanner—FIXD is optimized for consumer-friendly code reading, not shop-level diagnosis

    Common complaints

    Premium features require an ongoing subscription

    The free app functionality is genuinely limited. Severity ratings and repair cost estimates—the features that make FIXD useful versus a $20 code reader—are behind a subscription. Total cost is $89 sensor + $80–$120/year premium.

    Source: Reddit r/MechanicAdvice

    Stays plugged in draws minor battery power

    The sensor is always powered from the OBD2 port. In vehicles with known parasitic drain issues or those left parked for extended periods, some users report the always-on draw contributing to battery depletion over weeks.

    Source: Amazon.ca reviewer

    Doesn't replace a professional scanner

    FIXD covers basic engine codes well but misses ABS, SRS, transmission, and body module faults that many vehicles generate. Mechanics note it provides a false sense of complete diagnostic coverage to non-technical owners.

    Source: Reddit r/MechanicAdvice
    ClearPick Verdict

    FIXD is the right OBD2 tool for non-mechanical car owners who want to understand a check engine light before going to a mechanic and avoid being oversold on urgency. The plain-language explanations and severity ratings are genuinely valuable for the target audience. Factor in the Premium subscription if you want the full feature set. Mechanically inclined DIYers who want to actually diagnose and fix problems should step up to the Autel MK808—FIXD's consumer focus works against you when you need real data.
