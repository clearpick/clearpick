    ✅ What Works

    • Five temperature presets cover the full range of hot drink preparation — 160°F for green tea (which burns and becomes bitter at boiling), 175°F for white tea and delicate senncha, 185°F for oolong, 190°F for French press coffee, and 212°F for full boiling. Having accurate preset temperatures replaces the guessing game of letting boiling water cool down.
    • At $39 CAD, the Hamilton Beach delivers the core function of a variable-temperature kettle — accurate presets, fast boiling, and Keep Warm — at a fraction of the $150+ price of Fellow Stagg and similar premium models. For tea and coffee drinkers who want correct temperatures without spending premium pricing, this is the value entry point.
    • Boil time from cold to 212°F with a full 1.7L is approximately 7 minutes at 1500W — fast enough that daily use doesn't require planning ahead. For half-capacity (4 cups), boil time drops to under 4 minutes, which is genuinely quick for morning routines.
    • The stainless steel interior prevents plastic taste transfer that affects cheaper plastic-lined kettles. Both the body and lid interior are stainless, which is not universal at this price — many budget kettles use plastic interior surfaces that impart taste to the water over time.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Temperature accuracy varies ±5°F from preset values, which is adequate for tea and coffee but not for precision applications like specialty tea ceremonies or laboratory use. Premium kettles like the Fellow Stagg EKG maintain ±2°F. At $39, the variance is acceptable and real-world invisible in a cup.
    • The Keep Warm function holds temperature for 30 minutes. After 30 minutes the kettle shuts off, requiring a reheat cycle. If you steep multiple pots of tea over an extended afternoon and want continuous warm water, this requires attention.
    • The exterior plastic housing is not stainless steel — only the interior is stainless. The body is BPA-free plastic which is common at this price but means the exterior isn't as premium in feel as the interior quality suggests. The lid and handle are also plastic.
    • The water level indicator is on the side of the kettle and somewhat difficult to read when the kettle is on the base. A clearer or dual-side marking would improve ease of filling to an exact level — you end up estimating the fill more than reading a precise line.
