    ✅ What Works

    • GrillGrates work by concentrating heat into the raised rails, which reach temperatures 100–150°F hotter than the actual flame below. The result is grill marks and crust development on steaks and burgers that is genuinely better than what standard grill grates produce — especially on gas grills that don't naturally get hot enough for a proper sear. If you've ever been disappointed by a pale, steamed burger off a gas grill, GrillGrates solve exactly that problem.
    • The hard-anodized aluminum conducts and distributes heat far more evenly than the cast iron or stainless grates that come standard on most grills. Cold spots and hot spots largely disappear — you get consistent results across the entire cooking surface, which matters when you're feeding a crowd and need everything to finish at the same time.
    • The reverse flat side turns your grill into a quasi-griddle surface for delicate foods like fish fillets, shrimp, cut vegetables, and anything else that would fall through standard grates. Being able to flip between configurations mid-cook without any additional equipment is genuinely useful.
    • They work on any grill — gas, charcoal, pellet — and can be cut to fit unusual grill shapes. They also travel well, meaning you can bring your own grates to a cottage or tailgate and get consistent results regardless of what grill is available.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • GrillGrates require a different cleaning approach than standard grates. The channels between the rails trap drippings that should be scraped out while still hot using the included GrateTool. Letting them cool with accumulated grease makes cleanup harder. They're not dishwasher safe — hand scrape and wipe only.
    • The 13.75" × 16" total cooking area may not cover the full grill surface of a larger gas grill. You may need to buy multiple sets or a custom-sized set to cover a 4- or 5-burner grill completely. Measure your grill grate opening before ordering.
    • Aluminum conducts heat differently than cast iron — it heats up faster and responds to temperature changes more quickly, which is good for control but means it can also cool faster if your flame drops. On very low settings, the grates may not maintain the same radiant heat as a heavy cast iron grate.
    • These are an upgrade accessory, not a necessity. If your current grill already has thick cast iron grates and you're happy with your sear results, GrillGrates offer marginal improvement. They deliver the biggest benefit on gas grills with thin stainless grates or pellet grills that don't get hot enough to sear well by default.
