    ✅ What Works

    • Genuinely 300+ exercises from a single anchor point — rows, push variations, pistol squats, core work, and single-leg movements all transfer well, making it one of the most exercise-diverse pieces of equipment per dollar
    • Under 1 lb and fits in a mesh bag smaller than a water bottle — easier to pack for travel than resistance bands, and can hang from any door, beam, squat rack, or tree branch
    • Cam buckle adjustment system is fast and repeatable — swapping from a squat to a row to an inverted row takes seconds without re-anchoring
    • Build quality is noticeably superior to knockoffs: the stitching, cam buckle durability, and foam grip have a 10-year warranty behind them and have been tested to military and commercial gym standards

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • $250 CAD is a premium over knockoff suspension trainers that sell for $30–60 — the main argument for TRX is stitching/buckle durability over years and the warranty, but entry-level users doing light training may find a decent knockoff adequate
    • Door anchor is the most convenient setup, but not all doors work well — hollow-core doors or doors with strong drafts can be problematic; a ceiling-mounted TRX X-Mount ($40 CAD extra) is the preferred permanent solution
    • No dedicated padding on the straps themselves means prolonged contact on skin during certain exercises (like hip hinge movements) can be uncomfortable
    • The learning curve is steeper than machines or dumbbells — beginners benefit most from following the included video program or structured TRX app workouts rather than improvising

    ClearPick Verdict
