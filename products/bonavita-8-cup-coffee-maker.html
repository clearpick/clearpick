    ✅ What Works

    • The Bonavita achieves 92°C–96°C brew temperature, the SCA-certified optimal range for coffee extraction, using a 1500W showerhead that distributes water evenly across the entire coffee bed — the two most important variables in automatic drip coffee quality. Most sub-$100 machines never reach optimal temperature or distribute water unevenly. This one does both correctly.
    • The pre-infusion bloom feature mimics pour-over technique: it wets the coffee grounds for 20–30 seconds before full brewing begins, allowing CO2 to escape from fresh coffee and improving extraction. The difference is most noticeable with freshly roasted beans and produces coffee noticeably more complex than machines that skip bloom.
    • With one button and a single on/off switch, the Bonavita has the simplest control scheme of any quality coffee maker at this price. There's nothing to configure, no LCD to navigate, no app to pair with. You fill the water, put in coffee, and press the button. It makes excellent coffee immediately.
    • The stainless thermal carafe keeps coffee hot for 3–4 hours without a hot plate, which means no burnt, stale carafe coffee. The insulation quality is genuine — coffee at hour two tastes nearly identical to coffee at minute two, which is not true of the glass-and-hot-plate designs on budget machines.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The carafe has a narrow neck and awkward lid-removal design that makes cleaning the interior difficult. A bottle brush is required. Calcium and coffee oils build up inside over time and affect taste if not cleaned monthly. The carafe is not dishwasher safe.
    • The Bonavita is a one-task machine — it makes drip coffee, that's it. No programmable brew timer, no grinder, no espresso, no single-serve mode. For buyers who want to wake up to coffee already brewed, this isn't it. An outlet timer is the workaround.
    • Compared to the Moccamaster at $399, the Bonavita costs $300 less and produces coffee that most people can't distinguish in a blind taste test at home. At competitive cuppings, differences emerge. For daily home use, the gap is minimal.
    • The thermal carafe pours somewhat slowly and can drip around the spout if tilted too aggressively. It's an older carafe design and newer competitors have better pour geometry. A small inconvenience for what is otherwise an excellent machine.
