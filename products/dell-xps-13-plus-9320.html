    ✅ What Works

    • 13.4" OLED display with true blacks and vibrant color — one of the best screens available in a 13-inch Windows laptop, ideal for photo and video work.
    • Exceptionally thin and rigid CNC aluminum chassis — the XPS 13 Plus feels like a premium product that justifies its price in build quality alone.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Only two Thunderbolt 4 ports — no USB-A, no SD card, no HDMI. A hub is mandatory for any external peripherals.
    • Battery life trails MacBook Air significantly — 10–12 hours in light use vs. the Air's 18 hours. Heavy use drops it to 6–8 hours.

    Common complaints

    Poor battery life for the price

    At nearly $1,800, many users expect MacBook Air-level endurance. The XPS 13 Plus delivers 8–10 hours in real use — good but not exceptional.

    Source: Reddit (r/laptops)

    Capacitive function row is divisive

    The touch-sensitive row replacing physical F-keys is easy to accidentally activate and lacks tactile feedback. Polarizing for users who rely on F-keys in coding or gaming.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Runs warm under load

    The thin chassis limits cooling; sustained CPU workloads cause the bottom to become uncomfortably warm and trigger throttling.

    Source: Reddit (r/laptops)
    ClearPick Verdict

    The Dell XPS 13 Plus is the best-looking Windows ultrabook with one of the finest OLED displays in its class. Battery life and port selection are its main weaknesses — if you need all-day runtime without a charger, the MacBook Air is a better choice. If Windows is non-negotiable and aesthetics matter, the XPS 13 Plus delivers.
