    ✅ What Works

    • Note: The slug ryobi-pcl540b-cordless-circular-saw reflects this page's URL identifier; the product reviewed and sold here is the Ryobi PBLCS300B (ASIN B099CHFN88), an 18V HP 7-1/4-inch brushless circular saw — not the PCL540B rotary cut-out tool. The PBLCS300B uses the brushless HP motor series for superior runtime and power versus Ryobi's standard ONE+ tools.
    • The brushless HP motor is the upgrade that distinguishes this saw from the standard Ryobi ONE+ circular saws. Brushless motors generate less heat, run longer per battery charge, and maintain consistent speed under load better than brushed alternatives. In practice, this means cutting through 3/4-inch plywood at the end of a battery charge cuts as cleanly as at the beginning — something brushed motors don't consistently deliver.
    • A 56° bevel capacity is exceptional for a cordless circular saw in this price class, with most competitors topping out at 45°–52°. The wider bevel enables compound miter cuts and angled rips for stair stringer work, decorative trim, and compound angle joints that can't be cut on a standard 45° saw without multiple passes or a separate setup.
    • LED cut line illumination projects light onto the workpiece just ahead of the blade, making the cut line visible even in overcast outdoor conditions or dim interior workspaces without repositioning the workpiece or adding a separate light. Combined with the front sight window, the cutting position is clearly visible throughout the cut.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • This is a tool-only saw — no battery or charger included. Ryobi 18V ONE+ batteries range from $40 (1.5Ah) to $100+ (6.0Ah) on Amazon.ca. For a circular saw doing sustained cutting, a 4.0Ah or 6.0Ah battery is the appropriate match; smaller 1.5Ah and 2.0Ah batteries deplete quickly under the sustained load of rip cuts in plywood or dimensional lumber.
    • At a 2-1/4-inch maximum depth of cut at 90°, the PBLCS300B cuts 2× lumber (actual 1.5 inches thick) in a single pass with 3/4-inch clearance below the blade — appropriate for standard framing and sheet goods. It does not have the depth capacity to rip 4× lumber (3.5 inches thick) in a single pass; that requires a worm-drive or hypoid saw.
    • The carbide-tipped blade included with some PBLCS300B packages is a general-purpose construction blade. For finish plywood cuts — cabinet sides, drawer fronts, visible edges — a 40-tooth or 60-tooth fine-finish blade ($25–$40 CAD) dramatically reduces tearout and produces a much cleaner edge suitable for finish carpentry.
    • The saw is rated for the Ryobi 18V ONE+ battery platform, which is compatible with the standard ONE+ ecosystem but uses the HP brushless motor. HP brushless tools extract more power from the same battery than standard ONE+ tools — important context when comparing runtime to standard-motor ONE+ circular saws.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Ryobi PBLCS300B is the most capable circular saw in the Ryobi 18V ONE+ lineup, and the brushless HP motor gives it a genuine performance step over the standard brushed ONE+ model. For homeowners and light-duty contractors who want cordless freedom and already own ONE+ batteries, it's a compelling choice. The 2-1/4-inch depth capacity and tool-only format are the real constraints to budget for; pair it with a 4.0Ah+ battery and a finish blade for full usefulness.
