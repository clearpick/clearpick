    ✅ What Works

    • Broil King's Dual-Tube burner design is genuinely different from the competition. Each burner has two rows of ports rather than one, which means more even heat distribution and better flame consistency even in windy Canadian weather. The difference is noticeable when cooking a full rack of burgers — no cold spots in the middle.
    • The double-lined hood is a standout feature at this price point. It retains heat significantly better than single-walled competitors, which means faster preheat, better performance in cold weather, and more even ambient temperature for roasting and indirect cooking.
    • The lifetime burner warranty and 10-year hood/body warranty are the best in class at this price. Broil King backs these with actual service in Canada, and replacement parts are readily available. This is a grill you can buy knowing you won't be orphaned for parts in five years.
    • Reversible cast iron grates give you both a ribbed side for grill marks and a flat side for delicate fish, vegetables, and smaller items. The porcelain coating is thick and holds up well to regular use without chipping the way cheaper coatings do.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At 440 sq in primary cooking area, the Baron 420 is adequate for a family of four but can feel tight when entertaining. If you regularly cook for six or more people, consider the Baron 490 or Regal series for the extra square footage.
    • The 40,000 BTU main burner output is conservative compared to some competitors in this price range. This is a deliberate design choice that favors even heat over raw sear power — the grill excels at controlled roasting and consistent grilling but takes slightly longer to reach high-sear temperatures than high-BTU competitors.
    • Assembly takes 60–90 minutes for most buyers and requires two people to safely position the grill onto its cart. The instructions are clear but the process is time-consuming. Factor this in if you're buying as a gift or need it ready quickly.
    • Broil King is less common at Canadian mass-market retailers than Weber, so finding it in-store to see before buying can be challenging outside major cities. The Amazon.ca listing covers the return process if there are shipping damage issues.

    Common complaints

    Side burner igniter fails early

    Several buyers report the side burner electronic igniter stops working within the first season, requiring a manual lighter. The side burner itself works fine — it's only the ignition that fails.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Grease management could be better

    The drip pan and grease collection system requires more frequent emptying than expected during high-fat cooks. The tray slides out easily but the capacity is modest for a four-burner grill.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Assembly instructions have unclear steps

    Reddit users flag that a few assembly steps have ambiguous diagrams, particularly around the side shelf brackets. Taking photos of parts before assembly and reading ahead helps avoid confusion.

    Source: Reddit

    Price has crept up from launch

    Several reviewers note the Baron 420 was launched at a lower price point and the current pricing makes it feel less compelling versus Weber's Spirit II. The quality is there but the value proposition has narrowed.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

Ideal for home cooks who want a reliable everyday grill they won't have to replace in three years.
