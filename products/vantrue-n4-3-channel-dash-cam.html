    ✅ What Works

    • Three simultaneous channels (front exterior, interior cabin, rear exterior) provide the most comprehensive evidence coverage available—front captures approaching hazards and plate information, interior cabin camera records what happened inside the vehicle, rear captures rear-end collisions and tailgating; no single-camera or dual-camera alternative covers all three perspectives
    • The interior infrared cabin camera operates in complete darkness without visible light—critical for rideshare drivers who cannot illuminate the cabin during passenger trips, and for personal vehicles as a security deterrent that functions in dark garages
    • 4K front resolution at 30fps or 2.5K at 60fps mode provides user-selectable trade-off between maximum detail and smoother motion capture—2.5K 60fps is often preferable on high-speed highways where motion blur at 30fps reduces plate legibility at distance
    • 512GB microSD capacity support allows multiple days of continuous three-channel recording before loop overwriting, which is practically important for parking mode where days can pass between incident review

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Three simultaneous channels require significant microSD storage and generate large file sizes—a 128GB card provides approximately 3–4 hours of triple-channel recording before looping; a 256GB card is the practical minimum for daily driving
    • The interior cabin recording creates a privacy consideration for passengers who may not know they're being recorded; Canadian privacy law requires driver awareness that recording is occurring, and displaying a visible dash cam notification or informing passengers is advisable
    • The hardwire kit for parking mode is sold separately ($25–$35) and requires fuse box connection for constant power; without it, parking mode is not available and the camera powers off with the ignition
