    ✅ What Works

    • QLED quantum dot color at $499 CAD is genuinely impressive — colors are vivid and accurate in ways that edge-lit LED TVs at the same price can't replicate. Daylight viewing performance is strong, with high brightness that holds detail in brightly lit rooms.
    • Google TV is well-implemented and snappy on the S5 — apps launch quickly, the universal search works across all major Canadian streaming services, and Chromecast is built in. First-time smart TV buyers will find this much easier to navigate than Hisense's or Samsung's alternatives.
    • Setup is fast — TCL walks you through Wi-Fi, Google account, and streaming app setup in under 10 minutes with on-screen prompts. The remote is ergonomic and the quickstart guide is actually useful.
    • For a second TV, bedroom TV, or first 4K upgrade from an older set, the S5 represents extraordinary value. The picture quality difference from 1080p content on this screen is night-and-day, and the QLED color makes streaming look punchy and alive.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • No Mini-LED backlighting means local dimming performance is limited — dark scenes in movies will show more grey crush and less punch than the Q6 or competing Mini-LED models. The trade-off is the lower price.
    • Only one HDMI 2.1 port means only one device can run at 4K 120Hz — if you have both a PS5 and an Xbox Series X, one will be limited to 4K 60Hz or you'll need to swap cables.
    • 60Hz native panel is adequate for movies and casual gaming, but competitive gamers and sports fans accustomed to 120Hz will notice the difference on fast motion. This is expected at $499 but worth knowing before purchase.
    • The 35W speaker is thin on bass and doesn't fill larger rooms well — anyone watching in a room larger than 200 sq ft will benefit from even a basic soundbar. It's not bad, just limited by physics and the price point.
