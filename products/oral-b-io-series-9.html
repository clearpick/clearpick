    ✅ What Works

    • The iO Series 9's linear magnetic drive produces a smoother oscillation than the jerky motor motion of previous Oral-B generations. The result is effective cleaning with noticeably less vibration transmitted to the jaw, which matters significantly for users with sensitive teeth or dental work. This technology difference is real and consistent with dental professional reviews.
    • The AI Motion Sensor genuinely improves brushing behavior. In the app, you watch a real-time coverage map of your mouth fill in as you brush each zone — the visual feedback catches missed areas that 2 minutes of unsupervised brushing regularly skips. After 2–3 weeks of using the AI guidance, most users develop muscle memory for complete coverage even without the app.
    • The SmartPressure sensor stops the brush head from pulsating if you apply too much pressure, providing immediate tactile feedback. This is the most important feature for gum health — brushing too hard over years is the primary cause of gum recession, and this sensor breaks the habit.
    • Seven brushing modes (Daily Clean, Intense, Whitening, Sensitive, Super Sensitive, Tongue Cleaning, Gum Care) cover every brushing need in a single device. The modes aren't marketing differentiation; Sensitive and Super Sensitive genuinely reduce head speed and vibration to levels tolerable for recovering from dental procedures.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The AI features require the Oral-B app, which requires Bluetooth connectivity and periodic software updates. If you prefer a simple brushing tool without app dependency, the iO Series 9 is over-specified — the Oral-B Pro 1000 at $79 CAD delivers the same oscillating-rotating cleaning technology without the AI overhead.
    • Replacement brush heads for the iO Series run $8–15 CAD each and need replacement every 3 months per Oral-B's recommendation. Budget $30–60 CAD annually for heads, which is standard for premium electric toothbrushes but worth factoring into the total cost.
    • The magnetic charging case also functions as a travel case, which is a practical detail. The case charges the toothbrush and protects the head during travel — bring only the handle and case for trips, leave the charging stand at home.
    • Oral-B's oscillating-rotating-pulsating motion is clinically proven to remove more plaque than manual brushing and sonic brushes in head-to-head studies. Philips Sonicare (sonic technology) advocates claim gentler gum stimulation; the evidence favors Oral-B for plaque removal and Sonicare for sensitive gums. If gum sensitivity is your primary concern, look at the Sonicare lineup instead.
