    ✅ What Works

    • Vertical-first design is unique — the V10 is built for portrait orientation from the ground up, making vertical social media content natural rather than an afterthought. The sensor, menus, and stand all assume vertical shooting.
    • 1-inch sensor delivers a meaningful image quality jump over smartphone cameras — better low-light performance, more natural background separation, and significantly better colour rendering in difficult lighting.
    • Integrated fold-flat stand makes tabletop vlogging and desk streaming possible without buying a tripod or stand — the stand is sturdy enough for most casual use.
    • USB-C plug-and-play streaming: connect to a computer and it appears as a webcam immediately, with far better image quality than a typical built-in laptop camera.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Fixed 19mm-equivalent lens with no zoom — the V10 cannot zoom at all, optically or digitally in a useful way. If you need any reach beyond wide-angle, this camera is not the right choice.
    • 19mm-equivalent wide angle produces edge distortion — subjects at the frame edges appear stretched or warped, particularly noticeable for group selfies.
    • Battery life is short at ~200 shots — heavy video shooters will need a spare battery or USB-C power bank for all-day use.
    • At $499, the Sony ZV-1 II offers a zoom lens and is worth comparing — the V10's advantage is the vertical orientation and integrated stand rather than all-around capability.
