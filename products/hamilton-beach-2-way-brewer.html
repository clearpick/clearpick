    ✅ What Works

    • Two brew modes in one machine — full 12-cup carafe or single serve — eliminates the need for both a drip machine and a Keurig on the counter.
    • The single-serve side accepts K-Cup pods and ground coffee with the included reusable filter, which saves money on pods for buyers who already have ground coffee.
    • The programmable timer on the carafe side and the 2-hour auto shutoff make it low-maintenance for morning routines in shared households.
    • At $79 CAD, it costs less than many single-function coffee makers, making it the clearest value for households where different people want different brew volumes.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The single-serve side produces slightly weaker coffee than a dedicated Keurig — it runs cooler and faster, which reduces extraction for the same pod.
    • The carafe side only works with ground coffee — there's no pod option for full-pot brewing.
    • New machines have a noticeable plastic taste for the first five to ten brews that fades with use but can be off-putting initially.
    • The clock and programmable timer reset after any power interruption, even brief ones, requiring manual reconfiguration.
