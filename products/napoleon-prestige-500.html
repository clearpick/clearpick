    ✅ What Works

    • Napoleon's Wave grates are one of the most underrated grill features in the market. The wavy profile makes grill marks on food regardless of exact orientation, increases the contact area between grate and food, and distributes heat more evenly than straight bars. Combined with the porcelain-coated cast iron material, they develop and maintain heat in a way that produces noticeably better sear results than most competing grates.
    • The infrared rear burner is the feature that separates the Prestige 500 from the Broil King Baron at the same tier. At 18,000 BTU, the rear infrared burner reaches temperatures that conventional convection burners cannot, enabling a proper rotisserie sear and making this grill a genuine roasting machine. A whole chicken on the included rotisserie bracket comes out with even browning that gas grill owners rarely achieve.
    • Napoleon is designed and engineered in Barrie, Ontario, which matters practically in two ways: the design reflects Canadian climate considerations (cold weather performance, wind resistance) and warranty service happens through a Canadian company with domestic support infrastructure. The lifetime burner and cooking grate warranty is among the best in the category.
    • JETFIRE ignition lights each burner individually with a reliable spark rather than relying on shared ignition that can fail to light remote burners. It's a small thing but it works every time in cold weather when other ignition systems can be unreliable, and it means one failed igniter doesn't leave you fighting to light all four burners.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At $1,299 CAD, the Napoleon Prestige 500 is a premium purchase that competes with high-end Weber Genesis models. The build quality, grates, and infrared rear burner justify the price for buyers who use the grill seriously and regularly. For occasional weekend grilling, a $699 option delivers the same basic result for less money.
    • The infrared rear burner requires a rotisserie kit to use — the motor and spit rod are sold separately and not included in the base grill price. If rotisserie cooking is your reason for choosing this model, factor in the additional accessory cost.
    • The total BTU output (66,000 main + 10,000 side + 18,000 rear) is high and translates to real fuel consumption. A 20 lb propane tank will last fewer grilling sessions compared to lower-BTU alternatives. Keep a spare tank available if you cook frequently or for long sessions.
    • At 500 sq in primary cooking area, this is a generous grill for a family of four to six people. It's not the largest on the market — the Weber Genesis E-425s offers more square footage at a similar price — but most buyers find 500 sq in practical for real-world cooking without wasted surface.

    Common complaints

    Rotisserie kit sold separately

    The infrared rear burner — one of the flagship features — is only useful with a rotisserie kit that is not included with the grill. Buyers who purchase specifically for rotisserie capability are surprised by this and the additional cost.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Side shelf feels less premium than the rest

    Several reviewers note that the side shelves feel lighter and less solid than the rest of the build. It's a cosmetic and practical inconsistency on an otherwise premium-feeling grill.

    Source: Amazon reviewer

    Assembly takes 2–3 hours for one person

    Like most premium grills, assembly is complex and easier with two people. Some buyers report unclear instructions for the side burner and warming rack assembly steps specifically.

    Source: Reddit

    High price versus Weber for comparable cooking area

    At $1,299 CAD, some buyers feel Napoleon's premium pricing is harder to justify when Weber offers comparable cooking area and solid warranty at a similar or lower price. The Napoleon wins on the infrared rear burner and Canadian manufacturing — if those don't matter to you, the value equation shifts.

    Source: Amazon reviewer
    ClearPick Verdict

    The Napoleon Prestige 500 is the best gas grill for Canadian buyers who want premium construction, an infrared rear burner for serious rotisserie capability, and the peace of mind of lifetime warranty support from a Canadian company. It's priced at the high end of the mid-range segment and earns that price with the Wave grates, JETFIRE ignition, and exceptional cold-weather performance. If rotisserie cooking and Canadian-engineered quality matter to you, this is the grill.
