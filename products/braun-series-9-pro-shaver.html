    ✅ What Works

    • The 5-sync shaving elements work together in a way that no 3-element shaver can replicate — the ProLift Trimmer specifically targets flat-lying hairs that the foils can't catch, then cuts them before they reset. The result is noticeably fewer missed hairs and a closer shave on the first pass than the Series 7.
    • HyperLift technology lifts hairs up from the skin surface before cutting, which matters most for people with fine, flat, or short hair that lies flush against the skin. This is the specific reason dermatologists sometimes recommend the Series 9 for sensitive skin — the hair gets lifted and cut without the foil dragging against skin.
    • SkinGuard pressure sensor stops the motor from producing skin-irritating micro-cuts if you press too hard. Electric shaver beginners consistently apply too much pressure, and this safety net makes the learning curve significantly gentler.
    • Four weeks of use from a single 60-minute charge is accurate for most users shaving once daily. The battery life improvement over the Series 7 (3 weeks) reflects a larger cell, and you get a quick-charge feature that provides enough power for a single shave in 5 minutes.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • At $399 CAD, this is an investment that needs to be weighed against a lifetime of cartridge razor purchases. The math typically favors the Series 9 after about 2 years of use, but only if you actually commit to the electric shaver long-term — many people return to blades within 3 months.
    • The Pro Cleaning & Charge Station uses the same replacement cartridges as the Series 7 at similar cost. Braun has standardized this across the lineup, which is convenient but the cost remains $12–18 CAD per cartridge for regular users.
    • The 5-element head is noticeably larger than the Series 7 and some other shavers, which can make it slightly harder to navigate tight areas like under the nose. Some users prefer the Series 7's more compact head for this reason.
    • Braun recommends replacing the 5-element cassette every 18 months. The Series 9 Pro replacement cassette runs $60–80 CAD — higher than Series 7 due to the additional elements. Set a calendar reminder so you're not surprised.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Braun Series 9 Pro is the best foil electric shaver most people can buy — the 5-sync element system and HyperLift technology genuinely capture more hair in fewer passes than any 3-element competitor. For sensitive-skin users or anyone who's struggled with electric shavers missing flat-lying hairs, it's the clear recommendation. The $399 CAD price is objectively high and the Series 7 closes 80% of the performance gap at $150 less — but for the user who wants the absolute best electric shave and will keep it for 5+ years, the math works.
