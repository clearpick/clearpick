    ✅ What Works

    • The curved lounge shape gives cats a double-purpose object — a surface for scratching on the sides and a comfortable curved depression to sleep in on top. Most owners report their cats use it spontaneously for both functions without any training, and it frequently replaces a cat's favourite couch arm or carpet corner as the preferred scratch spot.
    • PetFusion uses recycled cardboard bonded with non-toxic corn starch glue — no adhesives that off-gas or create chemical exposure during scratching. For owners concerned about what their cats inhale while shredding cardboard, the material safety is genuinely better than cheaper alternatives using petroleum-based adhesives.
    • The reversible design meaningfully extends lifespan — when one scratching surface is worn down to smooth cardboard, flip the scratcher to expose a fresh surface on the underside. In practice this roughly doubles the usable life versus non-reversible scratchers, bringing the per-month cost well below cheaper alternatives.
    • Cat acceptance rate is extremely high, even for cats that have previously ignored or rejected other scratchers. The horizontal-to-angled surface matches the natural stretch-and-scratch posture cats use on furniture, which is likely why even scratch-resistant cats take to it. The included catnip speeds up introduction significantly.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Cardboard sheds flakes and dust during use — there will be cardboard debris around the scratcher, especially in the first few weeks when cats are working through the top layer. A mat underneath catches most of it, but it's a real consideration for owners who prefer tidy spaces.
    • The curved shape means this isn't a flat floor scratcher or a vertical post — cats that specifically prefer vertical scratching surfaces or a fully flat scratching pad may not use it the way you expect. Most cats adapt, but a few don't.
    • Replacement inserts are available from PetFusion, which is a better environmental and cost option than throwing away the entire frame. The outer shell remains intact long after the cardboard surface wears out.
    • At 34 inches long it's a substantial piece of furniture — it works well in a living room or dedicated cat space but is too large for small apartment bathrooms or laundry rooms where some owners try to hide cat furniture.
