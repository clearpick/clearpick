    What Buyers Say

    ✅ What Works

    • GS4 stainless steel burners light instantly via Infinity ignition every single time — no repeated clicking or manual lighting needed, a meaningful upgrade over ignitions found on cheaper grills
    • Excellent heat distribution across the full cooking surface eliminates cold spots; burgers, steaks, and chicken kebabs cook evenly without rotating, and the grill reaches mid-500°F for a proper sear
    • Flavorizer bars genuinely add smoky depth by vaporizing drippings on contact — particularly noticeable with fattier cuts like burgers and ribeye, where it noticeably edges out a plain gas grill
    • Weber's build quality is a tier above most competitors at this price: heavy-gauge sheet metal, porcelain-enamel hood that resists rust, and cast-iron grates that outlast the thin stamped steel found on $400 grills — many owners report 8–10+ years of service with basic maintenance

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • No side burner — a feature that's increasingly common even on budget grills; if you rely on a side burner for sauces, corn, or side dishes, look at the Weber Spirit II E-330 or a Broil King Signet model instead
    • Open undercarriage design means the propane tank is fully visible, which bothers buyers who want a clean patio aesthetic — the older Spirit E-310 (enclosed cabinet) or a cover solves this but adds cost
    • Assembly takes about an hour for two people (49 steps) — longer than competitors with simpler designs; the Bilt app with 3D animated instructions helps significantly and is worth downloading before you start
    • At $649 CAD on Amazon.ca, it costs more than similarly spec'd Broil King Signet models (which offer higher BTU output) — the Weber wins on long-term durability and brand support, but the price premium is real

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Weber Spirit II E-310 is the benchmark mid-range gas grill — not the flashiest or cheapest option, but the one most likely to still be running a decade from now.
