    ✅ What Works

    • The open-ear bone conduction design delivers full situational awareness while listening — you hear music and your environment simultaneously. For road runners, cyclists, and hikers, this is a safety feature that traditional earbuds fundamentally cannot offer.
    • The titanium frame is exceptionally durable and comfortable for extended wear. At 29g, these are barely noticeable during multi-hour runs — many users wear them all day without discomfort.
    • IP55 water resistance handles heavy sweating, rain, and splashes without issue. The proprietary magnetic charger is the only vulnerability, but the connector is robust in practice.
    • Quick charge is genuinely useful for athletes — 10 minutes on the charger gives 1.5h of playback, enough to get out the door without waiting. Full charge takes about 1.5 hours.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Bone conduction physics produce significantly less bass and lower maximum volume than in-ear headphones. If you're accustomed to in-ear buds, the audio quality will disappoint — this is a deliberate trade-off for open-ear awareness, not a product defect.
    • These are not suitable for noisy environments like gym floors, public transit, or busy offices — the open design means you hear everything around you. They're designed for outdoor use with light background noise.
    • The IP55 rating covers sweat and rain but NOT swimming. Despite the water resistance, these should not be submerged. Buyers wanting swim-proof bone conduction should look at the Shokz OpenSwim.
    • The proprietary magnetic charging cable is a dependency — losing it means ordering a replacement. It can't charge from a standard USB-C cable.
