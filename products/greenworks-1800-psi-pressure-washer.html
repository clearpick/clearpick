    ✅ What Works

    • At 9.4 lb, this is one of the lightest pressure washers available — significantly easier to carry, set up, and move around than heavier models. For users who want to clean multiple areas in one session, the portability difference is real.
    • Five quick-connect nozzles are included and cover the same range as pricier models — 0° to 40° and foam soap. The foam soap application is the most-used nozzle for car washing.
    • Compact enough to store in an apartment closet, condo storage locker, or trunk — a genuine advantage for urban Canadian buyers without a garage.
    • 1800 PSI is appropriate for cars, patio furniture, bicycles, lawn equipment, and light residential surface cleaning — the jobs most Canadian homeowners do most often.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • 1800 PSI and 1.1 GPM is the weakest among the three pressure washers reviewed — it will noticeably struggle on moderately soiled driveways, algae-covered surfaces, or anything requiring sustained scrubbing.
    • Single detergent tank vs Sun Joe's dual tanks is a meaningful limitation for car washing — you can't pre-load two different soaps.
    • Connections can loosen with repeated use over multiple seasons — an entry-level build-quality issue that emerges over time rather than immediately.
    • The 1.1 GPM flow rate means coverage is slower — you move the wand across the same area more times than a 1.76 GPM model.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The Greenworks GPW1803 is the right choice when portability is the primary requirement — 9.4 lb is genuinely light, and for apartment dwellers, condo owners, and buyers without a garage, that compact footprint matters. The 1800 PSI and 1.1 GPM limits are real: this is a maintenance cleaner for light tasks, not a heavy-duty surface cleaner. For buyers who want serious cleaning power, the Sun Joe SPX3000 adds 230 PSI and 0.66 GPM for $30 CAD more.
