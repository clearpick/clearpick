    ✅ What Works

    • Color E-ink at $159 is the lowest price for color e-paper — the Clara Colour brings the same panel technology as the Libra Colour in a smaller, cheaper package.
    • Native library borrowing via OverDrive/Libby — full Canadian public library integration at the lowest price point of any e-reader with this feature.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • 16GB storage is adequate for most readers — average e-book is 1–3MB; 16GB holds thousands of books. Only users who store audiobooks locally will need more.
    • 3-week battery is shorter than other options — smaller battery in the compact chassis means more frequent charging. A month of daily reading may require one or two charges.

    Common complaints

    3-week battery shorter than competitors

    The Clara Colour's 3-week battery vs. Libra Colour's 6 weeks means charging twice as often for heavy readers.

    Source: Reddit (r/ebooks)

    6-inch feels small for comics

    For manga and graphic novels, the 6-inch screen is cramped compared to the 7-inch Libra Colour. Text reading is fine at 6 inches.

    Source: Kobo review

    No physical page-turn buttons

    The Clara Colour is touchscreen-only. The Libra Colour has physical page-turn buttons — preferred by many e-reader users for one-handed reading.

    Source: Reddit (r/ebooks)
    ClearPick Verdict

    The Kobo Clara Colour is the best e-reader under $200 — color E-ink, library support, and open ePub format at $159. For heavy readers who want physical page buttons, step up to the Kobo Libra Colour at $60 more. For casual readers and library users, the Clara Colour is all you need.
