    ✅ What Works

    • The included divider panel makes the iCrate the best crate for puppies at this price — you start with a small section sized to the puppy and expand as they grow, rather than buying multiple crates or leaving a puppy in an oversized space that undermines house training. The divider is simple to reposition without tools.
    • Folding flat in under 30 seconds is genuinely useful for travel, apartment moves, or homes where the crate comes out only for specific situations. Unlike plastic travel crates that require full disassembly, the iCrate collapses into a flat panel and fits in a car trunk without dismantling.
    • The two-bolt latch system is more secure than single-bolt competitors at this price — dogs that have escaped from cheaper crates by nosing the latch typically cannot defeat the iCrate's dual-catch mechanism. This matters significantly for anxious dogs that push and test boundaries.
    • Heavy-gauge wire construction holds up to years of regular use without bending or warping, and the powder coat resists rust even in humid conditions. The MidWest warranty covers defects in materials and workmanship, and replacement panels are available — a product designed to last.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The 42-inch size is the right fit for most large breeds — Labs, Goldens, German Shepherds — but very large breeds (Great Danes, Irish Wolfhounds) need the 48-inch model. Measure your dog's standing height and length before ordering.
    • Single-door configuration means crate placement is limited — the door opens only from the front, which can be awkward in rooms where traffic flows from the side or back. MidWest makes a double-door version that adds this flexibility for a small premium.
    • The plastic floor tray can crack under heavy dogs or very active crating. Several owners of 90+ lb dogs report tray cracking within six months. A replacement tray costs about $20 and is worth having on hand, or placing a rubber mat inside to distribute impact.
    • Some dogs manage to move the crate across smooth floors because the rubber feet don't grip aggressively. A crate mat or furniture pad under the legs prevents this on hardwood or tile.
