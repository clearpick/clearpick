    ✅ What Works

    • The factory calibration to Delta E less than 2 means colors are accurate right out of the box — designers, photographers, and video editors can trust what they're seeing on screen without running separate calibration software.
    • The USB-C port with 65W Power Delivery handles both video signal and laptop charging in a single cable, making this an ideal dock-free setup for MacBook Air, Dell XPS, and Lenovo ThinkPad users who want a clean desk.
    • At 2560×1440, the WQHD resolution hits a sweet spot for 27-inch displays — sharp enough to read fine text and see detail in photos, while remaining less demanding on GPU resources than 4K at the same size.
    • The fully adjustable stand with height, swivel, tilt, and 90° pivot handles any ergonomic configuration, and the four USB-A hub ports on the back turn the monitor into a peripheral hub without requiring a separate dock.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • The 75Hz refresh rate is adequate for productivity work but falls short if you also want to use this monitor for gaming. Content creators who also game should look at gaming-oriented monitors or accept the compromise.
    • 65W USB-C charging is enough for ultrabooks and 13-inch laptops, but 15-inch and 16-inch laptops with discrete GPUs often need 90-100W to charge at full speed under load.
    • The IPS panel has the expected slight backlight bleed in very dark scenes, which affects movie watching in a dark room. For professional work in office lighting conditions, this is essentially invisible.
    • The ProArt Calibration software for fine-tuning color profiles requires a compatible colorimeter — the monitor doesn't include one. Delta E under 2 out of the box is enough for most professionals, but dedicated calibration hardware is a separate purchase.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The ASUS ProArt PA278CV is the best under-$600 monitor for creative professionals who need accurate color without the setup friction of manual calibration. Factory Delta E under 2 and 100% sRGB coverage mean you can trust the display on day one, and the USB-C with 65W Power Delivery makes this a one-cable desk for most laptops. The 75Hz refresh rate is the primary limitation for anyone who also games, but as a dedicated work monitor it's excellent.
