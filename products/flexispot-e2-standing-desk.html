    ✅ What Works

    • The dual-motor setup at this price point is a genuine value advantage — single-motor standing desks at similar prices are louder and tend to wobble more at standing height with heavier loads. The E2's dual motors distribute the lift evenly across both legs.
    • Four programmable height presets let you save your preferred sitting and standing heights, plus two additional positions for guests or different configurations. One press moves the desk from sitting to standing height without having to hold a button continuously.
    • The 28 to 47.6 inch height range covers the majority of users in both sitting and standing positions. Taller users above 6'2" may find the standing height slightly low, but the range is appropriate for average-height home and office users.
    • At $399 CAD, the E2 offers a complete sit-stand desk with dual motors, memory presets, and a full-size 48×24 inch desktop at a price that undercuts many competitors who offer equivalent specs at $500 or above in Canada.

    ⚠️ Worth Knowing

    • Assembly takes 30 to 45 minutes and requires a standard Phillips screwdriver. The instructions are functional but not exceptional — budget time for the first setup and consider having a second person help align the legs before tightening.
    • The 154-lb weight limit is per the desk frame and motors, not just the tabletop. A large monitor, desktop computer, multiple monitors, and accessories can approach this limit if you're not paying attention — weigh your intended setup before ordering.
    • Standing desk wobble at max height is a factor with all standing desks at this price range. The E2 is stable for typing and regular desk work, but the frame does flex slightly if you push on it at full standing height — the E7 Pro is meaningfully stiffer.
    • Some assembly reviewers note that leveling the desk on slightly uneven floors requires patience. The feet have limited adjustability, and on older homes with uneven flooring, shimming may be necessary.

    ClearPick Verdict

    The FlexiSpot E2 is the best-value entry into electric standing desks for Canadian buyers, offering dual-motor stability, four memory presets, and a full 48×24 inch desktop at $399. It won't win against the E7 Pro on rigidity or maximum standing height, but for most home office users who are new to sit-stand desks, the E2 provides the essential functionality at the lowest reasonable price. If you anticipate a heavy setup or are above average height, step up to the E7.
