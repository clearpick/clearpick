    Power Tools That Canadian DIYers and Pros Actually Rely On

    The tool market has consolidated around a few battery ecosystems — and the most important buying decision isn't which drill to buy, it's which 20V or 18V platform to commit to. DEWALT 20V MAX, Milwaukee M18, Makita 18V LXT, and Ryobi ONE+ each have strengths at different price and performance tiers. Once you're in an ecosystem, adding tools is cheap. Starting over is expensive.

    Our scores combine Amazon.ca ratings with feedback from r/DIY, r/Tools, professional trade reviewers at Pro Tool Reviews and ToolGuyd, and real-world durability reports from contractors. For Canadian buyers, we flag products that ship from US warehouses (potential customs delays and duty costs) vs. those available with Prime shipping from Canadian Amazon fulfillment centers.

    For corded vs. cordless, we address the actual tradeoff honestly: corded tools have unlimited runtime and consistent power, but cordless brushless motors have closed the gap significantly at normal DIY use. For measuring tools, we compare laser levels and laser measures against traditional alternatives and explain when the upgrade is genuinely worth it.
