Best Budget Ultralight Stove

BRS-3000T Titanium Stove

The BRS-3000T is the stove that defies its price tag. Weighing an almost unbelievable 26g (just under 1 oz) and constructed from titanium, it folds flat and screws onto any standard isobutane canister. For casual backpackers and ultralight enthusiasts who don't want to spend $70+ on a brand-name stove, the BRS-3000T delivers everything you need: a stable flame, standard canister threading, and a boil time comparable to far more expensive options.
Best Budget Mummy Bag

Coleman North Rim Adult Mummy Sleeping Bag

The Coleman North Rim is the go-to recommendation for anyone who needs a reliable cold-weather mummy bag without spending more than $100. Rated to -7°C, it uses Coleman's synthetic fill in a tapered mummy design that traps body heat efficiently. The integrated hood cinches down for extra warmth on colder nights, and the semi-flush zipper keeps drafts out. Coleman's quality control is consistent and well-tested — this bag reliably does what it says on the box.
Best Budget 2-Person Tent

Coleman Skydome 2-Person Tent

The Coleman Skydome 2-Person Tent is the best entry-level 2-person tent for Canadian campers — it sets up in under 5 minutes with Coleman's pre-attached poles, offers 20% more headroom than a standard Coleman dome tent, and includes a weather-resistant rainfly rated for moderate rain. For car camping and festival use, it's the most accessible quality tent available.
Best Integrated Cooking System

Fire-Maple Fixed Star X2

The Fire-Maple Fixed Star X2 is a self-contained cooking system combining an integrated canister stove with a heat-exchanger pot. The exchanger fins grip heat from the flame and transfer it to the pot far more efficiently than a standard stove — boiling 1L of water in just 2.5 minutes. Everything nests inside the pot for a compact, lightweight carry. A great pick for backpackers and solo campers who want speed and efficiency over fuel-carrying simplicity.
Best Traditional Camp Kettle

Kelly Kettle Trekker

The Kelly Kettle Trekker is the classic wood-burning camp kettle that has been boiling water in the Irish outdoors since 1896 — its chimney design boils water in under 4 minutes using just twigs, pine cones, or dry leaves. For backcountry Canadian campers who want to boil water without fuel canisters and appreciate the simplicity of a no-electronics, no-moving-parts design, the Trekker is legendary.
Best 3-Season Down Bag

Kelty Cosmic 20 Sleeping Bag

The Kelty Cosmic 20 punches well above its price for a down sleeping bag. Rated to -7°C (20°F), it uses 600-fill CloudLoft down insulation housed in a mummy-style shell that packs down to the size of a Nalgene. The semi-rectangular footbox allows natural foot movement, and the DriDown treatment helps the insulation maintain loft even in damp conditions. For 3-season Canadian camping without spending $400+ on a bag, this is the pick.
Best Cold-Weather Synthetic Bag

Marmot Trestles 15 Sleeping Bag

The Marmot Trestles 15 is a workhorse synthetic sleeping bag rated to -9°C (15°F) — practical for 3-season Canadian camping including chilly fall nights. Its SpiraFil synthetic insulation, unlike down, retains warmth even when wet — critical for damp coastal or shoulder-season camping. The Marmot Trestles line has a long reputation for durability and value, and the 15 hits the sweet spot for campers who need genuine cold-weather performance without the price or wet-weather risk of down.
Best Eco Sleeping Bag

Marmot Trestles Elite Eco 20

The Marmot Trestles Elite Eco 20 is the best sustainable sleeping bag choice for Canadian campers — it's made with recycled materials, rated to 20°F (-7°C) for shoulder season and early winter camping, and weighs only 1.4kg making it backpack-friendly. For campers who want performance credentials alongside genuine eco credentials, it's the standout in its price range.
Best 2-Person Backpacking Tent

MSR Hubba Hubba NX

The MSR Hubba Hubba NX has been the benchmark 2-person backpacking tent for years — and for good reason. Two doors and two vestibules mean both occupants can enter, exit, and store gear independently. The DAC Featherlight NSL poles are both light and strong. At 1.72 kg it's not the lightest tent ever made, but it's the sweet spot of livability, weather protection, and weight that most serious backpackers land on after trying the alternatives.
Best Canister Stove

MSR PocketRocket 2

The MSR PocketRocket 2 is the definitive ultralight canister stove — a benchmark in backpacking gear trusted by hikers worldwide. At just 73g it collapses to the size of a lighter, screwing directly onto standard isobutane/propane canisters. Its serrated pot supports hold pots of all sizes securely, and the precision valve gives you fine flame control from simmer to a rolling boil. If you only own one camping stove, this is the one.

The Solo Stove Lite is a compact, double-walled wood-burning camp stove engineered to produce a near-smokeless flame through its patented gasification design. Rated to -7°C with a brushed polyester lining, it's built for 3-season use and arrives with a compression sack. An honest, no-nonsense bag at a price that doesn't require a second mortgage.
